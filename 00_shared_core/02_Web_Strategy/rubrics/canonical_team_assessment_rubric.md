# Web Strategy Canonical Team Assessment Rubric

## Scope

This rubric applies to:

- team discussion submissions
- Brand Pod submissions
- paired-team discussion responses
- group case-study submissions

The same four criteria apply across assessment types. Expectations increase in depth, complexity, integration, and evidence from team discussions to group case studies.

The approved Canvas point values and weights remain authoritative. Do not infer or change point values from this file unless explicitly instructed.

---

## Criterion 1: Strategic Diagnosis and Critical Reasoning

Evaluates whether the team accurately identifies the central business problem, opportunity, tension, or decision and develops a logical strategic response.

Strong work:

- distinguishes root causes from visible symptoms
- identifies the most important strategic issue
- explains why the issue matters
- recognizes relevant tradeoffs, risks, and dependencies
- prioritizes rather than listing disconnected ideas
- recommends a feasible and specific course of action
- shows independent judgment rather than merely repeating the case or source

### Discussion expectation

Identify and analyze one primary issue, friction point, opportunity, or operating decision.

### Case-study expectation

Develop a more complete diagnosis that addresses causal drivers, strategic tradeoffs, risks, priorities, sequencing, and an integrated recommendation.

---

## Criterion 2: Application of Course Frameworks with Specific Examples

Evaluates whether the team correctly selects and applies relevant course and Overclocked frameworks.

Strong work:

- uses canonical framework names
- selects frameworks that fit the problem
- applies framework dimensions to specific facts or examples
- uses frameworks to advance the analysis rather than merely define terms
- connects multiple frameworks when the assignment requires integration
- demonstrates understanding of the framework's limitations and implications

### Discussion expectation

Correctly apply at least one primary course framework to the assigned digital surface, company, role, or decision.

### Case-study expectation

Integrate multiple relevant frameworks into the diagnosis, recommendation, implementation logic, and measurement approach.

---

## Criterion 3: Use of Examples and Evidence

Evaluates whether the team's claims and recommendations are supported with relevant, specific, and credible evidence.

Strong work may use:

- assigned case facts
- observed website, app, or digital-touchpoint evidence
- course readings and lectures
- industry research and data
- relevant company examples
- customer journey evidence
- metrics or proposed KPIs
- clearly labeled reasonable inference

Strong work distinguishes:

- **Observed:** directly visible or documented
- **Inferred:** a reasonable conclusion based on available evidence
- **Recommended:** the action proposed by the team

Do not treat AI output as evidence that a current company capability, system, or practice exists.

### Discussion expectation

Use a small number of highly relevant examples tied directly to the team’s diagnosis and recommendation.

### Case-study expectation

Triangulate multiple forms of evidence and explain how the evidence supports the strategic conclusion.

---

## Criterion 4: Writing Clarity, Organization, and Proper Citation of Course Concepts

Evaluates whether the team communicates its analysis clearly, professionally, and transparently.

Strong work:

- responds directly to the assignment
- uses a logical and easy-to-follow structure
- communicates concisely
- uses professional language
- names and attributes course frameworks accurately
- cites assigned and external sources appropriately
- discloses AI-tool use as required
- distinguishes the team's analysis from sourced or AI-supported material
- is proofread and submission-ready

### Discussion expectation

Provide a concise, organized response that makes the reasoning easy to follow and complies with assignment length and citation requirements.

### Case-study expectation

Produce an executive-quality argument with clear diagnosis, analysis, recommendation, implementation logic, and source attribution.

---

## Assignment Design Rule

Role lenses, website/app surfaces, metrics, paired-team replies, recommendation formats, and other assignment-specific requirements are not additional rubric criteria.

They are evidence students provide within the four canonical criteria.

Every graded assignment should include an instructor-facing alignment map showing which prompt requirements provide evidence for each rubric criterion.

Do not add, remove, rename, or reweight the four canonical criteria unless explicitly instructed by the instructor.
