# Web Strategy 360° Role-Coverage Audit

## Audit Scope

This audit reviews the proposed 360° Digital Operating Team Lens architecture in `course_design/module_role_crosswalk.md`. It does not revise the syllabus, alter assignments, change due dates or point values, add rubric categories, create Canvas pages, or resolve flagged inconsistencies.

## Overall Role Balance

The proposed rotation gives cumulative exposure to all seven operating lenses without forcing every role into every module:

- Digital Strategy / General Manager anchors leadership, strategy, business models, B2B platforms, media integration, GTM, innovation, AI, and final synthesis.
- Digital Product & Experience appears where customer jobs, adoption, disruption, infrastructure, consumer behavior, and innovation are central.
- Platform, Technology & Operations is concentrated in infrastructure, B2B platforms, measurement systems, and AI readiness.
- Analytics & Experimentation is present throughout strategy, insights, content effectiveness, measurement, innovation, and AI.
- Performance Marketing is concentrated in media, GTM, measurement, and campaign-facing applications.
- Digital Merchandising & Content is concentrated in business models, B2C/B2B presentation, content strategy, and media.
- CRM, Lifecycle & Loyalty is concentrated in insights, B2C behavior, media/GTM, measurement, and AI-enabled relationship contexts.

The architecture provides a credible 360-degree view because students encounter acquisition, merchandising, CRM, analytics, product, platform, and integration responsibilities across the semester.

## Overused Roles

**Digital Strategy / General Manager** is the most frequent lens. This is appropriate because the course is strategic and the role functions as the integration lens. Risk: students may produce generic executive advice. Future prompts should require a specific operating decision, dependency, tradeoff, and metric.

**Analytics & Experimentation** appears frequently because the syllabus emphasizes insights, measurement, optimization, AI, and evidence. Risk: students may overclaim from public information. Future prompts should require Observed / Inferred / Recommended distinctions.

## Underused Roles

**Digital Merchandising & Content** appears less in early modules. This is acceptable because the syllabus introduces content strategy later, but future Module 5 or Module 7 applications can preview merchandising through product pages, taxonomy, offers, or service presentation.

**CRM, Lifecycle & Loyalty** receives meaningful but not dominant coverage. It could be strengthened later through public loyalty pages, onboarding examples, app notifications, or instructor-provided email examples if workload allows.

## Performance Marketing Coverage

Coverage is strongest in:

- Module 9: Content Strategy, as a secondary channel and distribution lens.
- Module 10: The Digital Media Ecosystem, as a primary role.
- Module 11: Agile Go-to-Market Strategy, as a primary role.
- Module 12: Measurement & Integrated Analytics, as a primary role.
- Module 13: Innovation & Agile Marketing, as a secondary role.

This matches the syllabus sequence. Performance Marketing should not dominate early strategy modules except where campaign evidence clarifies a strategic assumption.

## Digital Merchandising & Content Coverage

Coverage is strongest in:

- Module 5: Digital Business Models.

- Module 9: Content Strategy.

This gives students meaningful coverage of assortment, taxonomy, search, browse, product presentation, offers, content hierarchy, and trust-building content without turning the course into production content design.

## CRM, Lifecycle & Loyalty Coverage

Coverage is strongest in:

- Module 6: Insights Driven Organizations.
- Module 7: B2C - The Digital Consumer.
- Module 10: The Digital Media Ecosystem, as lifecycle handoff.
- Module 11: Agile Go-to-Market Strategy.
- Module 14: AI & The Future of Digital, as a secondary relationship and personalization lens.

Students can learn identity, onboarding, retention, personalization, and loyalty using public or instructor-provided evidence. Private customer data should not be required.

## Analytics & Experimentation Coverage

- Module 6: Insights Driven Organizations.

This aligns with Insight Value Chain, Measurement Pyramid, Organizational Learning Loop, Kinetic Performance Framework, Reinvention Flywheel, Velocity Stack, and AI Enterprise Stack. The main risk is technical complexity, especially attribution and experimentation design.

## Digital Product & Experience Coverage

- Module 13: Innovation & Agile Marketing.

This helps students see websites, apps, account areas, support paths, and product pages as operating surfaces, not just communication channels.

## Platform, Technology & Operations Coverage

Coverage is strongest in:

- Module 4: Enabling Infrastructure.
- Module 8: B2B - Platforms and Marketplaces.
- Module 12: Measurement & Integrated Analytics.
- Module 14: AI & The Future of Digital.

This aligns with Technology-Organization-Environment Framework, Five-Layer Technology Stack, Industry Operating System Framework, Insight Value Chain, and AI Enterprise Stack. The main risk is unsupported claims about internal systems. Students should analyze visible dependencies and infrastructure categories, not proprietary stacks.

## Digital Strategy / General Manager Integration

The integration lens is strongest in:

- Module 1: leadership and landscape.
- Module 2: strategy assumptions.
- Module 3: disruption and reinvention.
- Module 5: business models.
- Module 8: B2B platforms.
- Module 10: media ecosystem and Domino's case context.
- Module 11: GTM and Nike case context.
- Module 13: innovation and Glossier case context.
- Module 14: AI and future readiness.
- Module 15: final synthesis, without forced application.

This is appropriate because the integration lens should reconcile specialized role recommendations, not replace them.

## Brand Pod Role-Pairing Difficulty

Balanced or manageable pairings:

- Module 1: Digital Strategy / General Manager vs Digital Product & Experience.
- Module 4: Platform, Technology & Operations vs Digital Product & Experience, if technical inference is scaffolded.
- Module 7: Digital Product & Experience vs CRM, Lifecycle & Loyalty.
- Module 9: Digital Merchandising & Content vs Analytics & Experimentation.
- Module 12: Analytics & Experimentation vs Performance Marketing, if attribution is simplified.

Higher-difficulty pairings:

- Module 8: Platform, Technology & Operations vs Digital Strategy / General Manager because B2B ecosystems are abstract.
- Module 14: Platform, Technology & Operations vs Digital Strategy / General Manager because AI infrastructure and governance can become speculative.

## Undergraduate Technical-Complexity Risks

Highest-risk modules:

- Module 4: infrastructure, security, and data flows.
- Module 8: B2B platforms and ecosystem coordination.
- Module 12: attribution and experimentation.
- Module 14: AI, governance, and enterprise capability models.

Controls:

- Use public surfaces only.
- Ask for infrastructure categories rather than vendor stacks.
- Require one decision, one dependency, and one metric.
- Require students to label observed evidence and reasonable inference.

## Asynchronous Workload Risks

Highest-risk weeks:

- Module 6: Midterm week.
- Module 10: Domino's case work begins and is due the following week.
- Module 11: Domino's and Nike case deadlines overlap.
- Module 13: Glossier case preparation and Case Study #3 due next week.
- Module 14: Case Study #3 due from the prior week.
- Module 15: Final review and final due date.

Role applications in these weeks should support existing assessments or remain optional. They should not add new graded workload without instructor approval.

## Alignment With Syllabus Objectives

The role rotation follows the syllabus arc:

- Modules 0-5: orientation, leadership, strategy, disruption, infrastructure, and business models.
- Modules 6-8: insights, B2C consumer behavior, and B2B platforms.
- Modules 9-12: content, media, GTM, and measurement.
- Modules 13-15: innovation, agile marketing, AI, future readiness, and final synthesis.

The strongest role applications are those that translate a module objective into an observable operating decision on a digital surface.

## Alignment With The Four Canonical Rubric Criteria

Role lenses remain evidence within the four canonical criteria:

1. Strategic Diagnosis and Critical Reasoning: roles clarify the operating decision, tradeoff, dependency, and recommendation.
2. Application of Course Frameworks with Specific Examples: roles give students a concrete way to apply Overclocked frameworks to a digital surface.
3. Use of Examples and Evidence: roles require observed evidence, reasonable inference, metrics, and public touchpoint analysis.
4. Writing Clarity, Organization, and Proper Citation of Course Concepts: roles can structure concise responses while preserving citation and AI-disclosure requirements.

No new rubric category is introduced or recommended.

## Digital-Career Awareness Opportunities

The role lens can help students see digital work as a cross-functional operating system:

- Product and experience work connects customer friction to adoption and value.
- Performance marketing connects acquisition, channels, campaign fit, and landing-page performance.
- Merchandising and content connect taxonomy, offers, presentation, and trust.
- CRM/lifecycle connects identity, onboarding, retention, personalization, and loyalty.
- Analytics connects instrumentation, dashboards, attribution, testing, and insight development.
- Platform and operations connect APIs, CMS, commerce, security, reliability, and resilience.
- Strategy/GM connects tradeoffs, sequencing, resources, and cross-functional alignment.

Career awareness should support, not replace, the course learning objectives.

## Strongest Module-Role Connections

- Module 4: Platform, Technology & Operations plus Digital Product & Experience makes infrastructure visible through customer trust and usability.
- Module 7: Digital Product & Experience plus CRM, Lifecycle & Loyalty turns consumer behavior into journey and relationship decisions.
- Module 9: Digital Merchandising & Content plus Analytics & Experimentation turns content strategy into business-value evidence.
- Module 10: Performance Marketing plus Digital Merchandising & Content plus Digital Strategy / General Manager fits owned, earned, and paid media orchestration.
- Module 12: Analytics & Experimentation plus Performance Marketing directly supports measurement, attribution, and testing objectives.
- Module 14: Platform, Technology & Operations plus Analytics & Experimentation plus Digital Strategy / General Manager turns AI into an enterprise capability question rather than a tool discussion.

## Modules Where Role Application Would Feel Forced

- Module 0: orientation should not require role analysis.
- Module 6: role work should be optional because the midterm is the stated assessment.
- Module 15: final review should use roles only as synthesis memory aids.

## 360-Degree Credibility Check

The proposed architecture creates a credible 360-degree view because it covers:

- Customer acquisition through Performance Marketing.
- Landing-page and conversion optimization through Performance Marketing, Product, and Analytics.
- Channel and campaign performance through Performance Marketing and Analytics.
- Assortment, taxonomy, search, browse, product presentation, and offers through Digital Merchandising & Content.
- Identity, onboarding, retention, personalization, and loyalty through CRM, Lifecycle & Loyalty.
- Instrumentation, dashboards, attribution, testing, and insight development through Analytics & Experimentation.
- Roadmaps, backlogs, UX, feature prioritization, and product adoption through Digital Product & Experience.
- Platforms, integrations, APIs, CMS, commerce, security, reliability, and resilience through Platform, Technology & Operations.
- Cross-functional prioritization, tradeoffs, sequencing, and resource allocation through Digital Strategy / General Manager.
