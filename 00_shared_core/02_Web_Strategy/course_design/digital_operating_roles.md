# Web Strategy 360° Digital Operating Role Library

## Purpose

This library defines the canonical Web Strategy operating-role lenses. These are not generic job descriptions. They are decision lenses that help students connect Overclocked frameworks, public digital touchpoints, customer outcomes, business outcomes, operating dependencies, and measurable performance.

Use these roles selectively. A normal weekly application should use no more than two or three primary roles, and only where the role lens advances the syllabus objective.

## 1. Digital Product & Experience

**Role purpose:** Translate customer needs, business priorities, and technical constraints into useful digital experiences.

**What the role owns:** Customer jobs, product or feature priorities, experience quality, roadmaps, requirements, usability tradeoffs, adoption, and the connection between customer friction and business value.

**Recurring decisions:** Which customer problem matters most; which feature, flow, or content area should change first; what belongs in the roadmap; what should be tested before launch; how much complexity to remove; what experience tradeoff is acceptable.

**Typical artifacts or work products:** Product brief, roadmap, backlog, journey map, feature hypothesis, requirements summary, prioritization matrix, launch checklist, customer-problem statement, experience audit.

**Primary customer outcomes:** Easier task completion, lower friction, clearer choices, stronger trust, better accessibility, smoother onboarding, and more useful self-service.

**Primary business outcomes:** Higher adoption, conversion, retention, satisfaction, repeat use, task success, support deflection, and customer lifetime value.

**Representative metrics:** Task completion rate, activation, conversion, adoption, repeat usage, drop-off, form completion, error rate, time to complete, support contact rate, customer satisfaction, app rating.

**Dependencies on other roles:** Depends on Analytics & Experimentation for evidence, Platform, Technology & Operations for feasibility and reliability, Digital Merchandising & Content for content clarity, CRM, Lifecycle & Loyalty for customer-stage context, Performance Marketing for traffic intent, and Digital Strategy / General Manager for prioritization.

**Relevant website, app, and digital-touchpoint responsibilities:** Onboarding, navigation, product or service flows, comparison tools, search and browse paths, account entry, checkout or application paths, app-store listing, help/support flows, product adoption surfaces.

**Common failure modes:** Treating product as visual design only; optimizing a screen instead of a journey; adding features without a customer decision; ignoring operational feasibility; mistaking preference for evidence.

**Relevant Overclocked frameworks:** Five Traits of Digital Leadership; Kinetic Strategy Framework; Five Catalysts of Disruption; Five-Layer Technology Stack; Consumer Journey Loop; Consumer Archetype Framework; Omni-Agent Framework; Reinvention Flywheel; Velocity Stack.

**Especially relevant Web Strategy modules:** Modules 1, 3, 4, 5, 7, 8, 13, and 14.

**Undergraduate-friendly examples:** Improve a product comparison page; reduce friction in a bank application entry point without logging in; evaluate an app-store listing; identify one customer task a loyalty page does or does not support.

**Common student misconceptions:** Product equals design; more features mean better strategy; usability is separate from business performance; back-stage systems do not shape front-stage experience.

## 2. Performance Marketing

**Role purpose:** Acquire qualified demand and improve conversion across paid, owned, earned, search, social, partner, and marketplace channels.

**What the role owns:** Campaign objectives, audience logic, channel mix, budget tradeoffs, creative testing, landing-page fit, acquisition efficiency, conversion path quality, and performance learning.

**Recurring decisions:** Which audience to reach; which channel fits customer intent; what message to test; where traffic should land; when to shift budget; how to balance reach, efficiency, and brand trust; when performance data is strong enough to act.

**Typical artifacts or work products:** Campaign brief, paid media plan, audience hypothesis, keyword or targeting plan, landing-page recommendation, creative test plan, funnel report, budget allocation summary, performance dashboard.

**Primary customer outcomes:** More relevant discovery, clearer offers, fewer mismatched clicks, useful landing experiences, and less intrusive or confusing messaging.

**Primary business outcomes:** Efficient acquisition, qualified traffic, stronger conversion, better lead quality, revenue growth, lower wasted spend, and improved go-to-market learning.

**Representative metrics:** Click-through rate, conversion rate, cost per acquisition, return on ad spend, cost per lead, lead quality, bounce rate, landing-page conversion, assisted conversion, search visibility, incrementality indicators.

**Dependencies on other roles:** Depends on Digital Merchandising & Content for message and page clarity, Digital Product & Experience for flow quality, Analytics & Experimentation for attribution and tests, CRM, Lifecycle & Loyalty for audiences and suppression logic, and Platform, Technology & Operations for page speed, tagging, and tracking reliability.

**Relevant website, app, and digital-touchpoint responsibilities:** Paid landing pages, search results, social ads, retail media placements, marketplace traffic, campaign URLs, app-install pages, product pages reached from campaigns, owned-media conversion paths.

**Common failure modes:** Optimizing clicks instead of outcomes; separating campaigns from landing-page quality; treating attribution as certainty; over-targeting without relevance; ignoring brand trust and customer experience.

**Relevant Overclocked frameworks:** Kinetic Strategy Framework; Consumer Archetype Framework; Content to Trust to Value Model; Media Ecosystem Framework; Paid Media Ecosystem Framework; GTM Orchestration Framework; Kinetic Performance Framework.

**Especially relevant Web Strategy modules:** Modules 9, 10, 11, 12, and 13.

**Undergraduate-friendly examples:** Compare an ad promise to its landing page; identify why campaign traffic might fail to convert; propose one metric for a paid campaign tied to a public product page.

**Common student misconceptions:** Performance means clicks; paid media can fix a weak offer; high reach equals strategy; attribution reports prove exactly what caused a sale.

## 3. Digital Merchandising & Content

**Role purpose:** Organize and present products, services, offers, and stories so customers can understand value, compare options, and take the next useful action.

**What the role owns:** Assortment presentation, taxonomy, search and browse logic, content hierarchy, offer clarity, product information, service explanation, content quality, trust cues, and content-to-conversion alignment.

**Recurring decisions:** What belongs on the page; how choices should be grouped; what offer or proof is missing; how search, browse, or product presentation should guide decisions; which content supports trust; where content should support conversion or loyalty.

**Typical artifacts or work products:** Content brief, product-page audit, taxonomy map, merchandising plan, offer matrix, content calendar, message hierarchy, product information checklist, search/browse recommendation.

**Primary customer outcomes:** Clearer understanding, easier comparison, stronger confidence, lower perceived risk, better findability, and more useful information at the point of decision.

**Primary business outcomes:** Higher conversion, average order value, product discovery, search success, content-assisted conversion, lower abandonment, stronger brand trust, and better offer performance.

**Representative metrics:** Product-page conversion, add-to-cart rate, offer selection, internal search success, category click-through, content-assisted conversion, scroll depth, product detail engagement, return/cancellation signal, assisted revenue.

**Dependencies on other roles:** Depends on Performance Marketing for traffic intent, Digital Product & Experience for page and flow structure, Analytics & Experimentation for content performance, CRM, Lifecycle & Loyalty for personalization and lifecycle context, and Platform, Technology & Operations for CMS, search, data feeds, and commerce systems.

**Relevant website, app, and digital-touchpoint responsibilities:** Product pages, category pages, service pages, search results, browse paths, comparison tables, product detail content, content hubs, help content, app modules, marketplace listings, email modules, social content.

**Common failure modes:** Treating content as decoration; writing for the brand instead of the customer decision; burying comparison information; measuring engagement without business relevance; ignoring inventory, eligibility, or operational constraints.

**Relevant Overclocked frameworks:** Business Model Evolution Framework; Consumer Archetype Framework; Content Strategy Pyramid; Content to Trust to Value Model; Agile Content Capability Matrix; Media Ecosystem Framework; Owned Media Architecture; Trust Amplification Model.

**Especially relevant Web Strategy modules:** Modules 5, 7, 8, 9, 10, and 11.

**Undergraduate-friendly examples:** Audit a product page for missing decision information; compare two category pages; explain how a content hub builds trust before conversion; inspect a loyalty page's offer clarity.

**Common student misconceptions:** Content strategy means posting often; merchandising applies only to stores; likes are the main content metric; a visually attractive page is automatically persuasive.

## 4. CRM, Lifecycle & Loyalty

**Role purpose:** Manage ongoing customer relationships across awareness, onboarding, use, retention, loyalty, reactivation, and advocacy.

**What the role owns:** Customer identity logic, lifecycle journeys, onboarding, segmentation, loyalty value proposition, email/SMS/app messaging, personalization rules, preference management, retention offers, and relationship measurement.

**Recurring decisions:** Which customer moment needs support; what message or offer is appropriate; when to personalize; when to suppress communication; how to build loyalty without weakening trust; how to move from transaction to relationship.

**Typical artifacts or work products:** Lifecycle map, onboarding journey, segmentation plan, loyalty journey, CRM campaign brief, trigger logic, retention dashboard, message calendar, personalization hypothesis, reactivation plan.

**Primary customer outcomes:** More relevant communication, smoother onboarding, useful reminders, clearer loyalty value, better recognition, fewer unwanted messages, and stronger relationship trust.

**Primary business outcomes:** Higher retention, repeat purchase, loyalty enrollment and participation, app engagement, customer lifetime value, reactivation, lower churn, and stronger first-party data value.

**Representative metrics:** Retention, churn, repeat purchase, loyalty enrollment, active loyalty participation, email open/click/conversion, unsubscribe, app engagement, reactivation, customer lifetime value, preference completion.

**Dependencies on other roles:** Depends on Analytics & Experimentation for segmentation and measurement, Platform, Technology & Operations for identity and data flows, Digital Merchandising & Content for message quality, Digital Product & Experience for account and loyalty surfaces, and Performance Marketing for acquisition-to-retention handoff.

**Relevant website, app, and digital-touchpoint responsibilities:** Loyalty explainer pages, preference centers, account areas, logged-out customer portals, onboarding flows, email, SMS, app notifications, post-purchase messages, reactivation pages, membership pages.

**Common failure modes:** Over-messaging; confusing personalization with relevance; treating loyalty as discounts only; ignoring consent and trust; failing to connect acquisition promises to post-purchase reality.

**Relevant Overclocked frameworks:** Insight Value Chain; Consumer Journey Loop; Consumer Archetype Framework; Omni-Agent Framework; Owned Media Architecture; Kinetic Performance Framework; AI Enterprise Stack.

**Especially relevant Web Strategy modules:** Modules 6, 7, 10, 11, 12, and 14.

**Undergraduate-friendly examples:** Evaluate a public loyalty-program page; map one onboarding need after app download; propose a retention metric without using private customer data; identify one personalization risk.

**Common student misconceptions:** CRM is only email; loyalty means points; personalization always improves experience; more customer data automatically creates better strategy.

## 5. Analytics & Experimentation

**Role purpose:** Turn data, observations, tests, and evidence into better decisions while acknowledging uncertainty and measurement limits.

**What the role owns:** Instrumentation logic, measurement plans, KPI definitions, dashboards, attribution interpretation, test priorities, learning agendas, insight translation, and evidence discipline.

**Recurring decisions:** Which metric fits the problem; what evidence is observable; what must be inferred; what test is feasible; how to interpret results; how to connect a learning to a business decision.

**Typical artifacts or work products:** Measurement framework, KPI tree, dashboard brief, test plan, learning agenda, attribution note, insight memo, experiment readout, metric-definition sheet, instrumentation requirements.

**Primary customer outcomes:** Better experiences because decisions are tied to observed friction, behavior patterns, tested improvements, and responsible interpretation.

**Primary business outcomes:** Faster learning, improved conversion, better resource allocation, reduced waste, clearer accountability, stronger adaptation velocity, and better decision quality.

**Representative metrics:** Conversion, completion, abandonment, cohort retention, funnel progression, test lift, confidence indicators, customer satisfaction, revenue per visit, attribution contribution, data-quality issue rate, learning velocity.

**Dependencies on other roles:** Depends on all roles for decision context. Especially depends on Platform, Technology & Operations for instrumentation, Digital Product & Experience for testable changes, Performance Marketing for channel data, CRM, Lifecycle & Loyalty for lifecycle data, and Digital Strategy / General Manager for prioritization.

**Relevant website, app, and digital-touchpoint responsibilities:** Analytics tags, public metric proxies, funnel checkpoints, campaign landing pages, product pages, content hubs, app-store signals, search behavior, A/B test plans, dashboard interpretation.

**Common failure modes:** Treating metrics as objectives; confusing correlation with causation; overclaiming from public evidence; ignoring data quality; selecting vanity metrics; assuming dashboards answer strategic questions automatically.

**Relevant Overclocked frameworks:** Insight Value Chain; Full-Spectrum Intelligence Model; Insight Maturity Matrix; Kinetic Performance Framework; Measurement Pyramid; Organizational Learning Loop; Reinvention Flywheel; Velocity Stack; AI Enterprise Stack.

**Especially relevant Web Strategy modules:** Modules 2, 6, 7, 10, 11, 12, 13, and 14.

**Undergraduate-friendly examples:** Choose one metric for a website friction point; propose an A/B test for a landing page; distinguish observed evidence from inference; explain what a dashboard would need to answer.

**Common student misconceptions:** Analytics is math detached from strategy; public observations prove internal performance; all metrics are equally useful; attribution is exact.

## 6. Platform, Technology & Operations

**Role purpose:** Ensure the digital business can run reliably, securely, scalably, and responsibly across systems, data, workflows, and customer touchpoints.

**What the role owns:** Platform capability, infrastructure reliability, integrations, APIs, CMS, commerce systems, identity/access patterns, data flows, operational resilience, governance, security, compliance support, and technical feasibility.

**Recurring decisions:** Which systems must connect; what must be reliable before launch; what risk must be reduced; which workflow dependency matters; what can be automated; where technical debt threatens customer or business outcomes.

**Typical artifacts or work products:** Capability map, dependency map, API or integration requirement, launch readiness checklist, risk register, data-flow sketch, operations playbook, service-level note, incident-learning summary.

**Primary customer outcomes:** Reliable access, fast response, secure transactions, trustworthy service, accurate information, fewer broken experiences, and better recovery from problems.

**Primary business outcomes:** Operational resilience, lower incident cost, scalable growth, improved data quality, faster delivery, compliance support, better integration, and stronger AI or analytics readiness.

**Representative metrics:** Uptime, latency, page load speed, error rate, authentication success, incident rate, recovery time, data-quality issue rate, release frequency, support escalation accuracy, checkout or application availability.

**Dependencies on other roles:** Depends on Digital Product & Experience for customer priorities, Analytics & Experimentation for instrumentation needs, CRM, Lifecycle & Loyalty for identity and customer data needs, Performance Marketing for traffic and tracking requirements, Digital Merchandising & Content for CMS and commerce needs, and Digital Strategy / General Manager for investment tradeoffs.

**Relevant website, app, and digital-touchpoint responsibilities:** Sign-in entry points, checkout or application availability, customer portals, commerce flows, CMS, APIs, personalization infrastructure, analytics tags, support workflows, search systems, app reliability, data feeds, disclosures.

**Common failure modes:** Treating infrastructure as invisible plumbing; naming vendor stacks without evidence; ignoring customer-facing consequences of back-stage failures; prioritizing tools over operating capability; treating security and compliance as obstacles rather than constraints.

**Relevant Overclocked frameworks:** Technology-Organization-Environment Framework; Five-Layer Technology Stack; Infrastructure Maturity Matrix; Insight Value Chain; Industry Operating System Framework; Velocity Stack; AI Enterprise Stack.

**Especially relevant Web Strategy modules:** Modules 4, 5, 6, 8, 12, 13, and 14.

**Undergraduate-friendly examples:** Map visible support friction to likely system dependencies; identify infrastructure categories needed for a bank application page; explain how app reliability affects trust.

**Common student misconceptions:** Technology is separate from strategy; students must know the exact internal stack; infrastructure matters only when something breaks; platform work is not customer work.

## 7. Digital Strategy / General Manager

**Role purpose:** Integrate customer, market, technology, financial, operational, and organizational perspectives into coherent digital choices.

**What the role owns:** Strategic diagnosis, prioritization, sequencing, tradeoff management, operating-model alignment, resource allocation logic, cross-functional synthesis, and business-level accountability.

**Recurring decisions:** What problem matters most; which opportunity fits the strategy; what tradeoff is acceptable; what sequence reduces risk; which roles must coordinate; what should be measured at the business level.

**Typical artifacts or work products:** Strategic diagnosis, decision brief, operating memo, initiative portfolio, roadmap rationale, executive recommendation, tradeoff matrix, capability gap analysis, transformation scorecard.

**Primary customer outcomes:** Coherent experiences across touchpoints, fewer broken promises, stronger trust, better alignment between brand promise and actual service.

**Primary business outcomes:** Competitive advantage, profitable growth, stronger retention, adaptability, velocity, operating alignment, better investment decisions, and improved resilience.

**Representative metrics:** Revenue growth, margin impact, retention, customer lifetime value, market share, initiative progress, kinetic score, speed-to-learning, adoption, risk reduction, portfolio performance.

**Dependencies on other roles:** Integrates all roles and depends on role-specific evidence. The integration lens should reconcile specialized recommendations rather than flatten them into generic strategy.

**Relevant website, app, and digital-touchpoint responsibilities:** Uses public digital surfaces as evidence of strategic choices, business-model design, channel integration, operating constraints, customer promise, and measurement priorities.

**Common failure modes:** Offering generic advice; ignoring execution dependencies; recommending too many priorities; skipping sequence and metrics; treating digital as a channel instead of an operating system.

**Relevant Overclocked frameworks:** Five Traits of Digital Leadership; Kinetic Strategy Framework; Five Catalysts of Disruption; Business Model Evolution Framework; GTM Orchestration Framework; Kinetic Performance Framework; Reinvention Flywheel; Velocity Stack; AI Enterprise Stack; Kinetic Economy Framework.

**Especially relevant Web Strategy modules:** Modules 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 10, 11, 13, 14, and 15.

**Undergraduate-friendly examples:** Prioritize one digital business-model opportunity; synthesize a case recommendation; explain how a brand should align content, media, technology, and measurement.

**Common student misconceptions:** Strategy means broad advice; executives do not need operational detail; a good idea is enough without dependencies; all recommendations can be pursued at once.
