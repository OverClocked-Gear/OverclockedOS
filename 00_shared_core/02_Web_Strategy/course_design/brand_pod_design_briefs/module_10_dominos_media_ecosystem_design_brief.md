## 2. Learning Purpose

Syllabus objective(s):

- Understand the relationship between owned media, content strategy, and customer empowerment.
- Create content strategies specifically designed to earn amplification.
- Develop an integrated paid media strategy that aligns with business objectives and connects effectively with content and earned media.

Primary Overclocked framework:
Owned/Earned/Paid media ecosystem, with owned media as the relationship foundation and paid/earned activity as traffic, trust, and amplification inputs.

Secondary framework(s), if needed:
Content strategy; adaptive media and measurement logic; customer journey / conversion path where appropriate.

Why this module needs a brand/surface application:
Module 10 moves students from content strategy into the larger media ecosystem. Domino's gives a case-adjacent context where students can test how a public digital surface might connect attention, offer clarity, owned experience, lifecycle relationship, and measurement without needing internal campaign data.

What students should understand after completing the activity:
Students should understand that media performance does not end at the click. Paid, earned, owned, content, CRM, and measurement decisions must connect around a customer job and a business outcome.

## 5. Surface Selection Rationale

Customer/user job:
Decide what to order, understand the offer, begin an order, evaluate value, understand a loyalty benefit, or move from paid/earned attention into an owned Domino's experience.

What the surface allows students to observe:
Message clarity, offer framing, product or menu presentation, calls to action, owned-channel prompts, evidence of media-to-owned transition, and public content that supports conversion or relationship development.

What the surface does not allow students to observe:
Paid media targeting, internal attribution, CRM rules, personalization logic, customer data, campaign performance, loyalty economics, internal analytics, or operational systems.

Why the surface is rich enough for Team A:
Team A can infer likely traffic intent, identify conversion or measurement friction, and propose how Performance Marketing and Analytics & Experimentation would connect attention to action.

Why the surface is rich enough for Team B:
Team B can evaluate content utility, merchandising clarity, offer explanation, lifecycle relationship cues, and how a public surface supports owned customer relationship development.

Evidence available to students:

- [x] visible page content
- [x] navigation or flow
- [x] trust/disclosure cues
- [x] product/service information
- [ ] support path
- [x] public app-store description
- [ ] instructor-provided screenshot/PDF
- [ ] instructor-provided data exhibit
- [x] assigned course material
- [ ] other:

Potential student overclaim risk:
Students may treat visible offers, page content, or app-store descriptions as proof of internal targeting, loyalty logic, campaign performance, or customer data use.

What students must not claim:
Students must not claim to know Domino's current paid media targeting, internal analytics, campaign performance, CRM rules, personalization logic, customer data, loyalty economics, vendor systems, or internal technology unless provided in assigned course material.

Why these role lenses fit the module:
Module 10 emphasizes owned, earned, and paid media integration. Team A examines acquisition, traffic intent, and measurement. Team B examines the owned surface, content usefulness, offer clarity, and relationship continuity.

Why the pairings are complementary:
Team A focuses on how attention arrives and how success would be measured. Team B focuses on what the customer needs once attention lands and how the relationship can continue after the visit.

Cognitive difficulty balance:

- [x] balanced
- [ ] Team A may need more scaffolding
- [ ] Team B may need more scaffolding
- [ ] both teams need scaffolding

Role-specific scaffolding needed:
Team A should avoid claiming actual targeting or performance data. Team B should avoid treating merchandising/content as visual design only or claiming internal CRM rules.

What each role owns:

Team A:
Performance Marketing owns traffic intent, media-to-landing fit, acquisition efficiency, and conversion path quality. Analytics & Experimentation owns KPI definition, instrumentation logic, test ideas, and evidence discipline.

Team B:
Digital Merchandising & Content owns product/offer presentation, content hierarchy, decision support, and content-to-conversion alignment. CRM, Lifecycle & Loyalty owns relationship continuity, opt-in/lifecycle expectations, loyalty value proposition, and retention logic.

What decision each role must make:

Team A:
What media-to-action improvement should be prioritized, and which metric would show whether attention converted into useful action?

Team B:
What content, offer, or lifecycle improvement would make the owned experience more useful and relationship-building?

Dependencies between roles:
Team A depends on Team B for message and offer clarity. Team B depends on Team A for traffic intent and measurement. Both depend on Product and Platform/Technology as implementation dependencies, but those are not primary lenses in this pilot.

## 8. Team A Task Design

Team A primary question:
How well does the assigned surface turn likely paid or earned attention into measurable owned action?

Team A should inspect:
The same assigned public Domino's surface and any instructor-approved supporting public evidence.

Team A should identify:

- likely traffic or attention source the surface is designed to receive
- one observed conversion, offer, clarity, friction, or measurement issue
- one dependency between media, landing experience, tracking, testing, or measurement

Team A should apply these framework(s):
Owned/Earned/Paid media ecosystem; paid media alignment; measurement and experimentation concepts.

Team A should recommend:
One specific improvement to better connect attention to action.

Team A metric family:
Click-to-action, landing-page conversion, offer selection, ordering-entry progression before login/payment, test lift, campaign-to-owned conversion, or another proposed metric matched to the issue.

Team A evidence requirement:
Observed public-surface evidence, reasonable inference about traffic or measurement needs, and one recommended action.

Team A common failure mode:
Claiming actual paid targeting, attribution, media spend, or campaign performance without evidence.

## 9. Team B Task Design

Team B primary question:
How well does the assigned surface use content, merchandising, and lifecycle cues to develop an owned customer relationship?

Team B should inspect:
The same assigned public Domino's surface and any instructor-approved supporting public evidence.

Team B should identify:

- what the customer needs to understand, compare, trust, or act on
- what Merchandising/Content owns and what CRM/Lifecycle owns
- one dependency between content, offer clarity, product presentation, lifecycle expectations, loyalty, or owned relationship development

Team B should apply these framework(s):
Content strategy; owned media; lifecycle and loyalty concepts; customer journey / owned experience connection.

Team B should recommend:
One specific improvement to strengthen the owned customer relationship.

Team B metric family:
Offer comprehension, product-page conversion, menu/product engagement, opt-in or loyalty-start progression where publicly appropriate, repeat-visit intent, content-assisted conversion, or another proposed metric matched to the issue.

Team B evidence requirement:
Observed public-surface evidence, reasonable inference about relationship continuity, and one recommended action.

Team B common failure mode:
Reducing the task to copy/design preference or making unsupported claims about loyalty, personalization, or CRM targeting.
