# Module 5 Instructional PowerPoint Outline: Digital Business Models — Deere and the Platform Shift in Precision Agriculture

Purpose: Create an instructor-facing outline for an explanatory PowerPoint/video that introduces Module 5 concepts, supports the Deere assignment, and helps students understand platform business models through a real-world precision-agriculture example.

Note: The video should teach platform surfaces and precision-agriculture examples as ways to understand business model evolution. The Module 5 discussion does not require students to access private Deere systems, download apps, log in, or use actual farm-management software.

## Slide 1: Module 5 Objectives

- Purpose: Introduce the learning goals.
- Key teaching points:
  - Business models explain how organizations create, deliver, and capture value.
  - Digital changes the logic of value creation.
  - This module asks students to identify business model opportunities within an existing organization and industry.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Three-part value flow: create, deliver, capture.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Connect the slide directly to the syllabus objectives.
- Connection to assignment: Frames Deere as the focal business model example.

## Slide 2: What a Business Model Is and Is Not

- Purpose: Prevent students from reducing the assignment to products or revenue.
- Key teaching points:
  - A business model is not just what a company sells.
  - It includes customers, value proposition, channels, relationships, revenue, resources, activities, partners, and cost logic.
  - Digital business models often turn products into ongoing systems of value.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Business model elements arranged around the customer job.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Emphasize that the assignment is not a company summary.
- Connection to assignment: Supports the pre-platform/post-platform comparison.

## Slide 3: Why Digital Changes Business Model Logic

- Purpose: Explain why the old business model may not be enough.
- Key teaching points:
  - Digital adds connectivity, data, software, workflow integration, and continuous service.
  - Value can move from one-time transactions toward recurring relationships.
  - Control can shift to whoever owns the decision layer, data layer, or workflow layer.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Product sale becoming a connected service loop.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Tie back to prior modules on strategy, disruption, and infrastructure without requiring a full prior-week analysis.
- Connection to assignment: Helps students discuss platform evolution without overloading the prompt.

## Slide 4: Hardware Model vs. Platform Model

- Purpose: Contrast the two business model logics.
- Key teaching points:
  - Hardware model: value centers on equipment performance, sales, service, parts, financing, and dealer relationships.
  - Platform model: value expands through connected machines, data, software, partner integrations, workflows, and ongoing decision support.
  - The platform does not replace the equipment; it changes what the equipment enables.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Side-by-side comparison table.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Use accessible language before introducing platform terms.
- Connection to assignment: Directly supports Deere before/after analysis.

## Slide 5: Deere Before Platform

- Purpose: Provide the pre-platform baseline.
- Key teaching points:
  - Deere can be understood through equipment, dealers, parts, service, financing, and machine performance.
  - Customer value centers on productivity, reliability, availability, service, and total cost of ownership.
  - The relationship is still ongoing, but the machine remains the primary value anchor.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Equipment-centered value chain with dealer/service support.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Do not suggest that this model is obsolete; it remains strategically important.
- Connection to assignment: Supports task 1.

## Slide 6: Deere After Platform

- Purpose: Explain platform-enabled precision agriculture.
- Key teaching points:
  - Connected equipment, Operations Center, precision ag, software, data, APIs, workflows, and partner ecosystem can expand Deere's role.
  - The strategic question shifts toward farm decisions, coordination, insights, and workflow support.
  - Students should cite current public claims with source/date and avoid unsupported feature claims.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Connected farm workflow with equipment, data, software, partners, and decisions.
- Optional instructor narration notes: State that students are not asked to access private tools.
- Connection to assignment: Supports tasks 2 and 8.

## Slide 7: Farmer / End-User Perspective

- Purpose: Center the customer job.
- Key teaching points:
  - Farmers evaluate whether the platform helps them make better operating decisions.
  - Potential value areas include productivity, ROI, coordination, uptime, ease of use, and decision confidence.
  - Potential concerns include trust, interoperability, switching costs, dependency, data use, and service friction.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Farmer job map from planning to field operation to analysis.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Push students to explain adoption conditions rather than assume adoption.
- Connection to assignment: Supports farmer/end-user analysis.

## Slide 8: Deere Product-Manager Perspective

- Purpose: Show how platform strategy changes management decisions.
- Key teaching points:
  - The product manager must decide what Deere should own, open, partner around, or protect.
  - Platform value may come from data gravity, workflow integration, ecosystem control, partner participation, and recurring relationship opportunities.
  - Openness and control are tradeoffs.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Product manager decision map: own, open, partner, protect.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Connect product decisions to value capture and farmer trust.
- Connection to assignment: Supports Deere product-manager analysis.

## Slide 9: Disintermediation Risk

- Purpose: Explain why continuous adaptation matters.
- Key teaching points:
  - Disintermediation means another actor captures the customer relationship, decision layer, data layer, or workflow layer.
  - Risks may come from startups, adjacent ag firms, input companies, autonomous tools, farm-management software, finance/insurance/data platforms, or other ecosystem participants.
  - Treat disintermediation as a strategic risk, not as proof Deere is currently being displaced.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Farm decision layer with multiple potential ecosystem owners.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Remind students not to speculate about unverified competitors or partnerships.
- Connection to assignment: Supports the disintermediation-risk task.

## Slide 10: Business Model Evolution Framework

- Purpose: Introduce the required primary framework.
- Key teaching points:
  - The framework explains movement from product/transaction logic toward subscription, marketplace, ecosystem, or generative-economy logic.
  - Students should use it to diagnose movement, not to force Deere into the highest stage.
  - Strong answers explain what changes and what remains important.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Maturity path from supplier/product logic to ecosystem/generative logic.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Emphasize judgment, not label matching.
- Connection to assignment: Students must apply this framework.

## Slide 11: Digital Business Model Canvas: What Changes

- Purpose: Show how to use the supporting tool selectively.
- Key teaching points:
  - Do not fill every canvas box in a short discussion.
  - Select two or three elements that changed most.
  - Likely areas include value proposition, customer relationship, channels, revenue streams, key resources, key activities, partners, communities, or data assets.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Business model canvas with highlighted changed boxes.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Model how one change can affect several other elements.
- Connection to assignment: Students must identify two or three changed elements.

## Slide 12: Metrics for Platform Business Models

- Purpose: Help students choose a matched metric.
- Key teaching points:
  - A metric should fit the recommendation.
  - Possible metric families include adoption, active use, workflow completion, retention, partner participation, support reduction, data-quality improvement, or task success.
  - A proposed metric does not need to be publicly available.
  - Avoid vague metrics unless the team explains exactly what would be measured.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Recommendation-to-metric matching table.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Explain why "engagement" is too broad unless defined.
- Connection to assignment: Supports the metric requirement.

## Slide 13: Evidence Discipline and AI/Source Transparency

- Purpose: Clarify evidence boundaries.
- Key teaching points:
  - Observed: sourced company facts, assigned course facts, or public Deere/platform evidence.
  - Inferred: reasonable business model or platform implications.
  - Recommended: the team's proposed business model move.
  - AI output is not evidence of current Deere features, partnerships, metrics, software revenue, farmer adoption, or internal strategy.
  - Current public claims need source/date.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Three evidence labels with AI outside the evidence box.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Encourage AI use for brainstorming or organization, not verification.
- Connection to assignment: Supports source and AI disclosure.

## Slide 14: Assignment Checklist

- Purpose: Give students a practical completion guide.
- Key teaching points:
  - Explain Deere before platform.
  - Explain Deere after platform.
  - Apply Business Model Evolution Framework.
  - Use Digital Business Model Canvas selectively.
  - Analyze farmer/end-user value and Deere product-manager tradeoffs.
  - Identify one disintermediation risk.
  - Name one public platform signal.
  - Recommend one business model move.
  - Propose one matched metric.
  - Add sources and AI disclosure.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Checklist matching the Canvas prompt.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Remind students not to log in, download apps, contact dealers, or use private tools.
- Connection to assignment: Directly mirrors the required response.
