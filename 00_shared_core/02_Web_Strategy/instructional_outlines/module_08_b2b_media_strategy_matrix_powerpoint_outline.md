# Module 8 Instructional PowerPoint Outline: B2B Buyer Behavior, Platforms, and the Media Strategy Matrix

Purpose: Create an instructor-facing outline for a short explanatory PowerPoint/video that introduces the Module 8 B2B assignment and helps students apply the Media Strategy Matrix to buying committees, platform value, proof, and lead nurture.

## Slide 1: Module 8 Focus

- Purpose: Introduce the B2B buyer and platform lens.
- Key teaching points: B2B digital behavior involves committees, risk reduction, education, proof, and ecosystem/platform value; the same matrix from Module 7 now applies to a different audience.
- Suggested visual or diagram: B2B buyer journey from awareness to consensus to qualified next step.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Emphasize that B2B is not simply a slower B2C purchase.
- Connection to assignment: Sets up the Salesforce analysis.

## Slide 2: Media Strategy Matrix Review

- Purpose: Reintroduce the shared framework.
- Key teaching points: The matrix uses awareness and purchase/evaluation intent; Module 8 shifts intent from individual purchase to organizational evaluation.
- Suggested visual or diagram: 2x2 matrix with B2B labels.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Compare briefly to Module 7's consumer framing.
- Connection to assignment: Required framework.

## Slide 3: Four B2B Buyer States

- Purpose: Translate quadrants into B2B behavior.
- Key teaching points: Low/low may need education; low awareness/high intent needs credible orientation; high awareness/low intent needs relevance and business trigger; high/high needs proof, consensus, and next step.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Four quadrant cards with buyer questions.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Keep the focus on one quadrant.
- Connection to assignment: Helps students choose a buyer state.

## Slide 4: Why Salesforce Fits Module 8

- Purpose: Explain the brand choice.
- Key teaching points: Salesforce offers public B2B surfaces including product pages, industry solutions, customer stories, learning content, events, and contact-sales paths; these can show content, proof, and platform positioning.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Public B2B surface map.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Do not make current feature or performance claims without cited evidence.
- Connection to assignment: Establishes the B2B context.

## Slide 5: Buying Committee Needs

- Purpose: Scaffold committee-based B2B behavior.
- Key teaching points: Different stakeholders may need ROI, integration confidence, security/trust, user adoption, operational fit, or executive proof; students infer needs carefully.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Committee map with stakeholder concerns.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Remind students that committee details are inferred unless a source states them.
- Connection to assignment: Required buying committee analysis.

## Slide 6: Content Role in B2B

- Purpose: Show why B2B surfaces have different jobs.
- Key teaching points: Product pages orient; industry pages translate relevance; customer stories provide proof; research reports educate; learning content builds confidence; events/webinars nurture.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Surface -> content role -> buyer need table.
- Optional instructor narration notes: The task is not to summarize every surface.
- Connection to assignment: Required content role.

## Slide 7: Trust and Proof Requirements

- Purpose: Explain why proof matters more in B2B.
- Key teaching points: B2B buyers manage risk; proof may include customer examples, implementation clarity, ecosystem credibility, security language, or business outcome evidence; students should not invent results.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Trust/proof ladder.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Separate public proof from private sales materials students cannot access.
- Connection to assignment: Required proof analysis.

## Slide 8: Lead Nurture Without Form Submission

- Purpose: Keep the activity safe and public.
- Key teaching points: Students can infer a next step without submitting forms; nurture can include educational content, webinar follow-up, comparison content, stakeholder materials, or consultative next-step messaging.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Public touchpoint -> possible nurture path.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Explicitly prohibit demo request submission, sales contact, and account creation.
- Connection to assignment: Required lead-nurture path.

## Slide 9: Platform and Ecosystem Context

- Purpose: Connect Module 8's platform objective.
- Key teaching points: B2B platform value can involve integrations, partner ecosystems, learning communities, workflows, and coordination; Platform/Technology is a dependency, not the assigned primary role.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Platform ecosystem ring around buyer organization.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Avoid technical architecture depth.
- Connection to assignment: Optional support for stronger B2B reasoning.

## Slide 10: Recommendation and Metric Fit

- Purpose: Connect B2B diagnosis to action and measurement.
- Key teaching points: Recommendations should improve the chosen buyer state; metrics may include qualified lead rate, demo-page progression, content-assisted conversion, webinar registration, customer-story click-through, sales-qualified handoff, or stakeholder engagement.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Buyer problem -> improvement -> metric.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Metrics may be proposed; students do not need proprietary analytics.
- Connection to assignment: Required recommendation and metric.

## Slide 11: Evidence Discipline and AI Transparency

- Purpose: Prevent overclaiming.
- Key teaching points: Observed means public/course-stated; Inferred means reasonable buyer or nurture logic; Recommended means the team action; AI is not evidence of current brand strategy or performance.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Evidence label table with B2B examples.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Students should cite current public claims with source and date.
- Connection to assignment: Required evidence/source discipline.

## Slide 12: Module 7 / Module 8 Comparison

- Purpose: Reinforce the paired sequence.
- Key teaching points: Same framework; B2C uses consumer motivation and lifecycle; B2B uses buying committee, proof, lead nurture, and platform risk; metrics and evidence burden differ.
- Suggested visual or diagram: B2C versus B2B side-by-side comparison.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Close by reminding students to analyze one quadrant deeply.
- Connection to assignment: Helps students transfer the framework without flattening B2B/B2C differences.
