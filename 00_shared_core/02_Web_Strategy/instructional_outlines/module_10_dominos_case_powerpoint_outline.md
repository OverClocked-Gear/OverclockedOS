# Module 10 Case Overview PowerPoint Outline: Domino’s, Digital Media, and Owned Customer Relationships

Purpose: Create an instructor-facing outline for a case overview video that prepares students for the Domino’s case without giving them a model answer.

Explicit note: The video should prepare students to frame the case and choose frameworks. It should not provide a model answer or reveal grading anchors.

## Slide 1: Module 10 Objectives

- Purpose: Introduce the module learning goals.
- Key teaching points:
  - Owned media, content strategy, and customer empowerment are connected.
  - Earned amplification depends on content, experience, and trust.
  - Paid media should connect to owned experience and business objectives.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Three-part objective map: owned, earned, paid.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Frame the case as media ecosystem strategy, not a discussion prompt.
- Connection to the case assignment: Establishes why Domino’s is analyzed through Chapters 10-12.

## Slide 2: Why Domino’s Is a Digital Media Ecosystem Case

- Purpose: Explain the case fit.
- Key teaching points:
  - Domino’s customer relationship is shaped by ordering, delivery/pickup, communication, loyalty, and repeat behavior.
  - Media channels influence how customers discover, choose, order, and return.
  - Platform pressure changes who owns the customer relationship.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Domino’s ecosystem map with owned, earned, paid, and third-party platform nodes.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Avoid adding facts not in the assigned case.
- Connection to the case assignment: Frames the central strategic problem.

## Slide 3: Owned Media, Content Strategy, and Customer Empowerment

- Purpose: Define owned media in the case context.
- Key teaching points:
  - Owned media includes channels the brand controls, such as website, app, email, SMS, push, or content surfaces.
  - Owned channels can reduce friction and strengthen direct relationships.
  - Customer empowerment means customers expect convenience, control, transparency, and relevance.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Owned-channel customer path.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Students should not log in, order, or use private account access.
- Connection to the case assignment: Supports owned-channel and journey analysis.

## Slide 4: Earned Amplification

- Purpose: Explain earned media as trust and reach.
- Key teaching points:
  - Earned media includes external attention, sharing, advocacy, reviews, and social conversation.
  - Useful content and strong experiences can generate amplification.
  - Earned media is valuable but less controllable than owned media.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Experience -> share/review/coverage -> trust loop.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Keep this conceptual unless assigned case evidence supports a specific example.
- Connection to the case assignment: Supports the optional Content → Trust → Value or media ecosystem analysis.

## Slide 5: Paid Media and Owned Experience Handoff

- Purpose: Show paid media as part of the journey, not a standalone tactic.
- Key teaching points:
  - Paid media can create reach, demand, or reactivation.
  - Paid media should hand off to a strong owned experience.
  - Weak handoff can waste acquisition spend or push customers toward external platforms.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Paid impression -> owned ordering path -> repeat behavior.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Do not make campaign-performance claims.
- Connection to the case assignment: Helps students connect paid media to business model and journey.

## Slide 6: Business Model Evolution Framework

- Purpose: Introduce the required synthesis framework.
- Key teaching points:
  - Business model evolution asks how value creation and value capture change.
  - Domino’s case can be read through owned relationships, channel control, ordering behavior, loyalty, data, and platform pressure.
  - The framework should organize the executive recommendation.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Value creation / value capture table.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Students do not need a full Business Model Canvas unless assigned separately.
- Connection to the case assignment: Required framework.

## Slide 7: Domino’s Owned Customer Relationship

- Purpose: Explain why direct relationships matter.
- Key teaching points:
  - Direct relationships can support customer experience control, retention, loyalty, feedback, and repeat behavior.
  - External platforms may provide reach but can weaken ownership of the customer relationship.
  - The case asks how to defend and extend direct relationships without ignoring market behavior.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Direct relationship versus mediated relationship.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Avoid overstating what Domino’s currently knows about customers.
- Connection to the case assignment: Supports diagnosis and recommendations.

## Slide 8: Customer Journey

- Purpose: Give students a practical journey lens.
- Key teaching points:
  - Analyze attention -> order -> delivery/pickup -> loyalty -> repeat behavior.
  - Identify where the journey creates or loses value.
  - Link journey friction to media and business model choices.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Domino’s journey strip with value/friction markers.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Students may use public or case-described touchpoints without ordering.
- Connection to the case assignment: Required journey/media ecosystem analysis.

## Slide 9: Platform Pressure and Third-Party Delivery Risk

- Purpose: Clarify the strategic risk.
- Key teaching points:
  - Third-party platforms can mediate discovery, ordering, data, price comparison, and customer relationship.
  - Platform dependence can create margin, control, data, and brand-equity risks.
  - The question is not only whether platforms add distribution, but what they change strategically.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Platform mediation triangle: customer, Domino’s, aggregator.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Students should use case evidence rather than current unsupported claims.
- Connection to the case assignment: Required platform-pressure/disintermediation risk.

## Slide 10: Data and Insight Boundaries

- Purpose: Prevent unsupported claims.
- Key teaching points:
  - Observed means case facts, course materials, or approved public evidence.
  - Inferred means reasonable conclusions from available evidence.
  - Recommended means the team’s proposed priorities.
  - Students cannot claim internal data, attribution, segmentation, targeting, contracts, or systems.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Observed / Inferred / Recommended labels.
- Optional instructor narration notes: AI output is not evidence of current Domino’s capabilities or performance.
- Connection to the case assignment: Supports evidence discipline.

## Slide 11: Choosing Two Supporting Frameworks

- Purpose: Help students avoid framework overload.
- Key teaching points:
  - Choose frameworks that strengthen the recommendation.
  - Owned / Earned / Paid helps analyze media ecosystem.
  - Consumer Journey Loop helps analyze friction and repeat behavior.
  - Insight Value Chain helps analyze learning and feedback.
  - GTM Orchestration helps align channels and teams.
  - Content → Trust → Value helps if content or earned amplification is central.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Framework choice decision tree.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Strong work applies a few frameworks deeply.
- Connection to the case assignment: Supports required framework choice.

## Slide 12: Prioritizing Top Three Recommendations

- Purpose: Teach executive prioritization.
- Key teaching points:
  - Priorities should solve the diagnosed problem.
  - Priorities should work together, not as isolated tactics.
  - Strong recommendations explain sequencing, tradeoffs, and expected outcome.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Priority stack with dependencies.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Discourage “more app features” and “more social media” as generic answers.
- Connection to the case assignment: Required top-three priorities.

## Slide 13: Metrics That Fit the Recommendation

- Purpose: Connect recommendations to measurement.
- Key teaching points:
  - Metrics should match the priority and customer behavior.
  - Possible metric categories include owned-channel adoption, repeat ordering, loyalty participation, conversion, retention, customer journey completion, earned amplification, or paid-to-owned handoff quality.
  - Metrics may be proposed; students do not need proprietary analytics.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Recommendation -> behavior -> metric table.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Avoid presenting metrics as actual Domino’s data.
- Connection to the case assignment: Required two or three metrics.

## Slide 14: Case Submission Checklist

- Purpose: Give students a final completion guide.
- Key teaching points:
  - Executive diagnosis.
  - Business Model Evolution Framework.
  - Two supporting frameworks.
  - Customer journey / media ecosystem analysis.
  - Platform-pressure risk.
  - Top three priorities.
  - Metrics, conclusion, sources, and AI disclosure.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Checklist.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Remind students this is a group case, not a peer-reply discussion.
- Connection to the case assignment: Submission support.
