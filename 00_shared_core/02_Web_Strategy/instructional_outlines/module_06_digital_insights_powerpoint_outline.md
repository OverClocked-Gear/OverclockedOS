# Module 6 Instructional PowerPoint Outline: Digital Insights — Weaponizing Data

Purpose: Create a short explanatory PowerPoint/video introducing Chapter 6 and preparing students for the midterm without adding a new graded discussion.

Explicit note: Module 6 does not require students to become data scientists. It asks them to understand how data becomes insight and how insight changes strategy, customer experience, and business outcomes.

## Slide 1: Module 6 Objectives and Midterm Context

- Purpose: Position Chapter 6 as a learning bridge during midterm week.
- Key teaching points:
  - Module 6 focuses on insights-driven organizations.
  - The syllabus places the Quiz/Midterm in this module.
  - The warm-up is optional practice unless the instructor changes Canvas.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Course arc from Chapters 1-5 synthesis to Chapter 6 insight capability.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Emphasize that this material supports strategic thinking and midterm preparation.
- Connection to warm-up or midterm: Frames the warm-up as practice in turning evidence into decisions.

## Slide 2: What It Means to Think Like a Data Strategist

- Purpose: Define the managerial skill students are practicing.
- Key teaching points:
  - A data strategist does not simply ask for more data.
  - The strategic question is what decision needs better evidence.
  - Good insight work connects customer behavior, business action, and measurable outcomes.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Decision-first funnel: decision -> evidence need -> signal -> insight -> action.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Stress that students are not being asked to build models or code analytics systems.
- Connection to warm-up or midterm: Helps students explain why a signal matters.

## Slide 3: Data vs. Signal vs. Insight vs. Decision

- Purpose: Give students plain-language distinctions.
- Key teaching points:
  - Data is raw information.
  - A signal is a meaningful pattern or clue.
  - An insight explains what the signal means for a customer or business decision.
  - A decision is what the organization changes because of the insight.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Four-column progression with a simple customer-behavior example.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Use a simple example such as repeated skipped recommendations becoming a product or messaging decision.
- Connection to warm-up or midterm: Directly supports the required signal -> insight -> decision logic.

## Slide 4: Dark Data and Lost Opportunity

- Purpose: Explain why unused data creates strategic drag.
- Key teaching points:
  - Dark data is collected or generated but not used effectively.
  - Unused data can create lost opportunity, slower learning, and weaker customer understanding.
  - The goal is not collecting everything; the goal is activating the right evidence.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Storage pile versus illuminated decision path.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Keep the explanation business-focused rather than technical.
- Connection to warm-up or midterm: Helps students diagnose weak insight capability as a strategic problem.

## Slide 5: Insight Value Chain Overview

- Purpose: Introduce the core Chapter 6 framework.
- Key teaching points:
  - The Insight Value Chain turns data into organizational action.
  - Appendix B components: Capture, Integrate, Analyze, Synthesize, Plan, Execute, Measure.
  - A break in any link can slow insight velocity.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Chain diagram with seven links.
- Optional instructor narration notes: If using a six-step teaching version, clarify the instructor's preferred wording before publishing.
- Connection to warm-up or midterm: The warm-up asks students to apply the chain to one signal and one decision.

## Slide 6: Stitch Fix — Data as the Fabric of the Business

- Purpose: Use the Chapter 6 anchor example.
- Key teaching points:
  - Stitch Fix illustrates data as a source of personalization and business-model defensibility.
  - Customer signals can include preferences, feedback, kept/returned items, fit, style notes, or timing.
  - The strategic value comes from improving decisions, not from data collection alone.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Customer feedback loop from profile/preferences to recommendation to outcome.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Avoid unsupported current claims; keep the example anchored in assigned Chapter 6 material.
- Connection to warm-up or midterm: Provides the primary brand context for the optional activity.

## Slide 7: Spotify as a Familiar Comparison Example

- Purpose: Give students a second accessible personalization context.
- Key teaching points:
  - Spotify can be used as a familiar public example of recommendation, discovery, retention, and listening-experience logic.
  - Possible signals include skips, repeats, saves, playlist behavior, or listening context.
  - Students should not claim to know Spotify's internal algorithms or private metrics.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Listening signal -> recommendation/discovery decision -> engagement outcome.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Make clear whether Spotify is assigned course material or only a familiar comparison.
- Connection to warm-up or midterm: Supports comparison without requiring current company research.

## Slide 8: How Insights Show Up in Customer Touchpoints

- Purpose: Make abstract insight work visible through digital experience examples.
- Key teaching points:
  - Insights often appear as recommendations, onboarding questions, preference settings, homepage modules, re-engagement messages, or product discovery paths.
  - A touchpoint is where a strategy becomes visible to the customer.
  - Students should analyze public or course-described touchpoints only.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Customer journey strip with insight-enabled moments highlighted.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Remind students not to log in, download apps, or inspect private account experiences.
- Connection to warm-up or midterm: Supports the required touchpoint connection.

## Slide 9: Cross-Functional Activation

- Purpose: Show that insight becomes value through organizational action.
- Key teaching points:
  - Analytics & Experimentation interprets signals and measures results.
  - Digital Product & Experience turns insight into product or journey changes.
  - CRM, Lifecycle & Loyalty turns insight into retention, personalization, and relationship decisions.
  - Operations and strategy determine feasibility and priority.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Cross-functional activation map around one insight.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Keep role language light so it supports rather than expands the activity.
- Connection to warm-up or midterm: Helps students see insight as an operating system, not a dashboard.

## Slide 10: Metrics — Knowing Whether Insight Changed Outcomes

- Purpose: Teach matched measurement.
- Key teaching points:
  - A metric should match the decision being improved.
  - Possible metrics include engagement, repeat use, retention, conversion, recommendation acceptance, task completion, or reactivation.
  - Metrics may be proposed; students do not need access to proprietary analytics.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Decision-to-metric matching table.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Warn against vague metrics such as "more engagement" without defining the behavior.
- Connection to warm-up or midterm: Supports the required metric or success signal.

## Slide 11: Evidence Discipline and AI/Source Transparency

- Purpose: Prevent overclaiming and AI misuse.
- Key teaching points:
  - Observed means course-stated or publicly visible.
  - Inferred means a reasonable conclusion from what is observed.
  - Recommended means the student's proposed decision, action, or metric.
  - AI output is not evidence of current company algorithms, systems, metrics, or performance.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Three evidence labels with examples and non-examples.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Reinforce that students remain responsible for accuracy and majority authorship.
- Connection to warm-up or midterm: Aligns with the course-wide evidence and AI transparency rules.

## Slide 12: Midterm Bridge — From Insight to Strategy

- Purpose: Connect Chapter 6 to strategic diagnosis and recommendations.
- Key teaching points:
  - Chapter 6 can strengthen a strategic recommendation by identifying what the organization needs to learn faster.
  - Insight capability can support adaptability, retention, business model evolution, and customer experience decisions.
  - Strong midterm answers prioritize causal drivers rather than listing every framework.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Strategic recommendation supported by evidence, insight, implementation, and metric.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Do not provide a model answer. Keep the review at the level of method and reasoning.
- Connection to warm-up or midterm: Helps students use Chapter 6 without disrupting the existing midterm structure.
