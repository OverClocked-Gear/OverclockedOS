# Module 11 Case Overview PowerPoint Outline: Kraft Heinz, Agile@Scale, and GTM Velocity

Purpose: Create an instructor-facing outline for a case overview video that prepares students for the Kraft Heinz case without giving them a model answer.

Explicit note: The video should prepare students to frame the case and choose frameworks. It should not provide a model answer or reveal grading anchors.

## Slide 1: Module 11 Objectives

- Purpose: Introduce the Module 11 learning goals.
- Key teaching points:
  - Design GTM strategies that integrate digital and traditional channels.
  - Create adaptive processes for continuous strategy refinement.
  - Evaluate organizational structures for effective GTM execution.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Objectives mapped to strategy, process, and structure.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Note that this proposed package uses Kraft Heinz as a replacement case while the syllabus still references Nike.
- Connection to the case assignment: Frames the case around GTM execution rather than a discussion prompt.

## Slide 2: Why Kraft Heinz Is a CPG Agile GTM Case

- Purpose: Explain the case fit.
- Key teaching points:
  - CPG companies must coordinate insight, product development, retail, media, and commercialization.
  - Mature brands can struggle when consumer tastes and retail expectations shift faster than planning cycles.
  - Agile@Scale makes organizational design part of the GTM strategy.
- Suggested visual or diagram: CPG GTM system map.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Avoid unsupported current Kraft Heinz claims.
- Connection to the case assignment: Establishes the case context.

## Slide 3: The Problem With Traditional CPG Planning Cycles

- Purpose: Show why agility matters.
- Key teaching points:
  - Long stage-gate cycles can create slow handoffs.
  - Functional silos can separate consumer insight from commercialization.
  - Speed without learning can create waste; learning without execution can create delay.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Linear stage-gate path versus agile loop.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Do not include the 36-month-to-6-month timeline unless source support is confirmed.
- Connection to the case assignment: Supports the central GTM diagnosis.

## Slide 4: Agile@Scale and Cross-Functional Pods

- Purpose: Introduce the operating-model concept.
- Key teaching points:
  - Cross-functional pods bring key roles closer together.
  - Pods can reduce handoffs and shorten feedback loops.
  - Scaling pods requires governance, prioritization, and shared metrics.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Pod diagram with marketing, R&D, commercialization, analytics, retail activation, and feedback.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Keep pod discussion conceptual unless assigned case materials provide specifics.
- Connection to the case assignment: Required pod / GTM process analysis.

## Slide 5: Agile Marketing Operating System

- Purpose: Explain the required operating framework.
- Key teaching points:
  - An agile marketing operating system organizes how teams sense, decide, launch, measure, and iterate.
  - The goal is not just more speed; it is faster learning tied to business outcomes.
  - Students should evaluate how the operating model creates or limits GTM performance.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Sense -> prioritize -> sprint -> launch -> measure -> adapt loop.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Confirm canonical naming before publishing if the latest appendix changes.
- Connection to the case assignment: Required framework.

## Slide 6: Kinetic Strategy — Velocity vs. Adaptability

- Purpose: Give students the second required framework.
- Key teaching points:
  - Velocity is how quickly the organization can move.
  - Adaptability is how well the organization can change direction based on learning.
  - The strongest GTM systems build both speed and adaptability.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Adaptability x Velocity quadrant.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Ask students to avoid treating speed alone as success.
- Connection to the case assignment: Required Kinetic Strategy analysis.

## Slide 7: Insight-to-Launch Loop

- Purpose: Show the operating flow students should analyze.
- Key teaching points:
  - Consumer signal becomes insight.
  - Insight shapes concept, product, message, and retail activation.
  - Market response should feed the next iteration.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Signal -> insight -> concept -> launch -> measure -> learn loop.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Connect to Insight Value Chain if students choose it as support.
- Connection to the case assignment: Helps students explain pod impact.

## Slide 8: GTM Orchestration

- Purpose: Show what must be coordinated.
- Key teaching points:
  - GTM requires coordination across R&D, marketing, analytics, commercialization, retail, and feedback.
  - Retail activation and shopper education are not separate from marketing strategy.
  - Integrated execution prevents pods from becoming isolated teams.
- Suggested visual or diagram: GTM orchestration wheel.
- Optional instructor narration notes: GTM Orchestration Framework is one possible supporting framework.
- Connection to the case assignment: Supports framework selection and recommendations.

- Purpose: Provide a placeholder for instructor-verified example support.
- Key teaching points:
  - Delimex / 360CRISP may be used as a proof-point example only if source support is confirmed.
  - The example should illustrate agile product development, launch coordination, or learning cycles.
  - It should not become a model answer.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Optional proof-point card with “verify before publishing” label.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Remove or revise this slide if the example is not included in the approved source packet.
- Connection to the case assignment: Optional source-supported example, not required student evidence unless assigned.

## Slide 10: Risks of Agile at Scale

- Purpose: Help students identify tradeoffs.
- Key teaching points:
  - Risks include fragmentation, governance gaps, brand inconsistency, speed without learning, duplicated work, and weak measurement.
  - Agile systems need guardrails.
  - Strategic discipline matters as much as speed.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Risk / guardrail table.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Encourage students to name one tension, not every possible risk.
- Connection to the case assignment: Required risk or tension.

## Slide 11: Choosing One Supporting Framework

- Purpose: Prevent framework overload.
- Key teaching points:
  - GTM Orchestration supports channel/team alignment.
  - Insight Value Chain supports learning and feedback logic.
  - Digital Horizon Planning supports current optimization versus future options.
  - Business Model Evolution supports value creation and capture.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Framework choice decision tree.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Strong case work applies a small number of frameworks deeply.
- Connection to the case assignment: Required supporting framework choice.

## Slide 12: Prioritizing Top Three Recommendations

- Purpose: Teach executive prioritization.
- Key teaching points:
  - Priorities should solve the diagnosed GTM problem.
  - The three priorities should work together as an operating system.
  - Strong recommendations explain sequencing, tradeoffs, and measurement.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Priority stack with dependencies.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Discourage generic “use more AI” or “post more content” recommendations.
- Connection to the case assignment: Required top-three priorities.

## Slide 13: Metrics That Fit Agile GTM

- Purpose: Connect recommendations to evidence of progress.
- Key teaching points:
  - Metrics should reflect speed, learning quality, and market impact.
  - Possible metric families include insight-to-concept time, concept-to-test time, concept-to-shelf time, launch learning-cycle time, retail sell-through, repeat purchase, forecast accuracy, promotion lift, and pod decision-cycle time.
  - Metrics may be proposed; students do not need proprietary dashboards.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Recommendation -> behavior -> metric table.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Avoid presenting metrics as actual Kraft Heinz data.
- Connection to the case assignment: Required metrics.
