# Module 13 Case Overview PowerPoint Outline: Glossier, Community, Innovation, and Agile Brand Reinvention

Purpose: Create an instructor-facing outline for a case overview video that prepares students for the Glossier case without giving them a model answer.

Explicit note: The video should prepare students to frame the case and choose frameworks. It should not provide a model answer, reveal grading anchors, or reproduce HBR case content.

## Slide 1: Module 13 Objectives

- Purpose: Introduce the Module 13 learning goals.
- Key teaching points:
  - Design customer-centric innovation processes that balance creativity with systematic execution.
  - Design agile marketing operations that balance flexibility with strategic consistency.
  - Develop data-driven optimization approaches for continuous improvement.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Objectives mapped to innovation, agile operations, and measurement.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Keep the case framed as Group Case Study #3, not a discussion prompt.
- Connection to the case assignment: Establishes the framework and assessment context.

## Slide 2: Why Glossier Is an Innovation and Agile Marketing Case

- Purpose: Explain the case fit.
- Key teaching points:
  - Glossier connects customer conversation, brand community, product development, and channel strategy.
  - The case surfaces the challenge of scaling a digitally native consumer brand.
  - The assignment asks students to design an innovation and agile marketing system, not just a campaign.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Community -> product -> content -> channel -> feedback loop.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Do not reproduce HBR case content or provide a model answer.
- Connection to the case assignment: Frames the central strategic question.

## Slide 3: Community-Led Brand Building

- Purpose: Clarify the value and limits of community.
- Key teaching points:
  - Community can support insight, trust, advocacy, and product relevance.
  - Community is not the same as follower count.
  - Community-led growth can create tension when the brand scales.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Community value / scaling pressure two-column chart.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Keep examples anchored in the assigned case or instructor-approved materials.
- Connection to the case assignment: Supports stakeholder and scaling-tension analysis.

## Slide 4: Innovation Full Flywheel / Reinvention Flywheel

- Purpose: Introduce the required synthesis framework.
- Key teaching points:
  - Appendix B confirms Reinvention Flywheel as the canonical Chapter 15 framework.
  - The flywheel helps students explain how insight becomes experimentation, scaling, and institutional learning.
  - The framework should organize the recommendation.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Sense -> Explore -> Experiment -> Scale -> Institutionalize.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Use the naming preferred in class.
- Connection to the case assignment: Required synthesis framework.

## Slide 5: Agile Marketing Operating System

- Purpose: Introduce the second required framework.
- Key teaching points:
  - Agile marketing connects cadence, testing, learning, and strategic consistency.
  - Agile does not mean chasing every trend.
  - Students should evaluate how Glossier organizes decisions and learning.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Plan -> sprint/test -> measure -> adapt loop.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Emphasize operating discipline, not just speed.
- Connection to the case assignment: Required framework.

## Slide 6: Customer Insight to Product/Experience Loop

- Purpose: Show the central operating loop.
- Key teaching points:
  - Customer signals can inform product, content, service, and channel decisions.
  - Insight must move into action and measurement.
  - Feedback should shape the next cycle.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Customer signal -> insight -> product/experience decision -> launch/test -> learning.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Connect to Insight Value Chain if students choose it.
- Connection to the case assignment: Supports framework selection and touchpoint analysis.

## Slide 7: Stakeholders

- Purpose: Help students identify tradeoffs.
- Key teaching points:
  - Customers/community may value intimacy and authenticity.
  - Product and innovation teams need actionable learning.
  - Content/community and CRM teams shape relationship continuity.
  - Analytics teams measure learning and performance.
  - Leadership and channel partners balance growth, control, and resources.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Stakeholder map.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Students should choose two or three key tensions, not every stakeholder.
- Connection to the case assignment: Required stakeholder lens.

## Slide 8: Surfaces Where Innovation Becomes Visible

- Purpose: Make innovation observable.
- Key teaching points:
  - Surfaces may include product pages, reviews, content/community, social surfaces, retail/channel touchpoints, lifecycle messaging, and feedback loops.
  - Students should distinguish HBR case facts from current public evidence.
  - No login, purchase, signup, or private account inspection is required.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Touchpoint map from discovery to feedback.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Do not require live private customer experiences.
- Connection to the case assignment: Required surfaces/touchpoints analysis.

## Slide 9: Scaling Tension

- Purpose: Name the strategic risks.
- Key teaching points:
  - Potential tensions include authenticity vs. growth, channel expansion vs. brand control, product speed vs. quality, personalization vs. trust, and community intimacy vs. operational scale.
  - Strong case work identifies one central tension and builds recommendations around it.
  - Not all growth options preserve the same customer relationship.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Tension matrix.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Avoid turning the case into a generic growth brainstorm.
- Connection to the case assignment: Required scaling risk or tension.

## Slide 10: Choosing One Supporting Framework

- Purpose: Prevent framework overload.
- Key teaching points:
  - Insight Value Chain supports customer learning and data-to-action logic.
  - Consumer Journey Loop supports customer experience and touchpoints.
  - Business Model Evolution supports channel and value-capture shifts.
  - Content → Trust → Value supports community/content and trust.
  - Kinetic Strategy supports adaptability and velocity.
  - Impact × Feasibility supports prioritization if approved.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Framework choice decision tree.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Strong work applies one support framework deeply.
- Connection to the case assignment: Required support-framework choice.

## Slide 11: Prioritizing Top Three Recommendations

- Purpose: Teach executive prioritization.
- Key teaching points:
  - Priorities should solve the diagnosed problem.
  - Priorities should work together as an innovation and agile marketing system.
  - Strong recommendations explain sequencing, stakeholder tradeoffs, and measurement.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Priority stack with dependencies.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Discourage generic “more social” or “more products” answers.
- Connection to the case assignment: Required top-three priorities.

## Slide 12: Metrics That Fit Innovation and Agile Marketing

- Purpose: Connect recommendations to measurement.
- Key teaching points:
  - Metrics should reflect learning, customer relationship, product adoption, and business outcomes.
  - Possible metric families include insight-to-test time, experiment learning velocity, repeat purchase, retention, feedback quality, launch sell-through, review quality, and channel contribution.
  - Metrics may be proposed if they are clearly tied to the recommendation.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Recommendation -> behavior -> metric table.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Avoid presenting metrics as current Glossier data.
- Connection to the case assignment: Required metrics.

## Slide 13: Evidence Boundaries

- Purpose: Prevent overclaiming and protect HBR content.
- Key teaching points:
  - Observed means HBR case facts, assigned course materials, or approved public evidence.
  - Inferred means reasonable conclusions from available evidence.
  - Recommended means the group’s proposed priorities.
  - The video and prompt should not reproduce HBR case content.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Observed / Inferred / Recommended labels.
- Optional instructor narration notes: AI output is not evidence of current Glossier capabilities or performance.
- Connection to the case assignment: Supports evidence discipline and source transparency.

## Slide 14: Case Submission Checklist

- Purpose: Provide a final completion guide.
- Key teaching points:
  - Executive diagnosis.
  - Innovation Flywheel.
  - Agile Marketing Operating System.
  - One supporting framework.
  - Stakeholder and touchpoint analysis.
  - Scaling risk or tension.
  - Top three priorities.
  - Metrics, conclusion, sources, and AI disclosure.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Checklist.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Remind students this is a group case, not a peer-reply discussion.
- Connection to the case assignment: Submission support.
