# Module 2 Instructional PowerPoint Outline: Strategy Reimagined — From Static Planning to Kinetic Strategy

## Slide 1: Why Strategy Needs an Upgrade

- Purpose: Introduce the central Module 2 problem.
- Key teaching points: Traditional strategy tools were built for slower, more stable markets; digital competition changes the clock speed; strategy now needs to support learning and movement.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Split screen: "Static Plan" vs. "Adaptive Operating System."
- Optional instructor narration notes: Emphasize that students are not discarding classic strategy; they are learning when static assumptions fail.
- Connection to assignment: Sets up why Adobe should be evaluated with Kinetic Strategy rather than a traditional SWOT.

## Slide 2: What Changes in Digital Competition

- Purpose: Explain the digital forces that disrupt traditional assumptions.
- Key teaching points: Compressed time horizons; porous industry boundaries; customer empowerment; cloud, SaaS, and API dynamics; lower switching and distribution friction.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Four-part wheel labeled time, boundaries, customers, infrastructure.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Keep examples general and avoid current company claims that would need verification.
- Connection to assignment: Helps students identify one traditional assumption Adobe challenged.

## Slide 3: Kinetic Strategy Framework Overview

- Purpose: Define the primary framework.
- Key teaching points: Kinetic Advantage = Velocity x Adaptability; organizations are evaluated by speed of learning/execution and capacity to reconfigure.
- Suggested visual or diagram: 2x2 matrix with Laggard, Stalwart, Explorer, Architect.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Stress that the matrix is diagnostic and directional.
- Connection to assignment: Students must use this framework as the main analysis.

## Slide 4: Velocity, Explained Simply

- Purpose: Make velocity undergraduate-friendly.
- Key teaching points: Velocity is not just "going fast"; it includes sensing, deciding, shipping, testing, updating, and learning.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Loop: signal -> decision -> release -> feedback -> next action.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Use familiar examples such as app updates or subscription service improvements.
- Connection to assignment: Students should assess how Creative Cloud changed Adobe's learning and update cycles.

## Slide 5: Adaptability, Explained Simply

- Purpose: Distinguish adaptability from velocity.
- Key teaching points: Adaptability is the ability to change the model, not just execute faster; it includes revenue model, customer relationship, resources, and operating assumptions.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Before/after blocks: product sale -> ongoing relationship; release cycle -> continuous service.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Explain that a company can be fast at an outdated model and still be strategically brittle.
- Connection to assignment: Students should assess how Adobe reconfigured the business model.

## Slide 6: Adobe Before Creative Cloud

- Purpose: Ground the case setup.
- Key teaching points: Boxed software depended on major releases, one-time transactions, retail/package logic, and long upgrade cycles; the model faced piracy and uneven revenue.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Timeline showing packaged release spikes.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Use assigned materials; do not add unverified current data.
- Connection to assignment: Students must explain Adobe's strategic problem.

## Slide 7: Adobe After Creative Cloud

- Purpose: Show the strategic shift.
- Key teaching points: Subscription model; continuous updates; recurring customer relationship; cloud storage/collaboration; greater usage and adoption visibility.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Continuous loop: subscribe -> use -> update -> learn -> improve -> renew.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Frame this as an operating-model change, not only a pricing change.
- Connection to assignment: Supports touchpoint and operating-implication analysis.

## Slide 8: Adobe Through Velocity and Adaptability

- Purpose: Model framework application without giving an answer key.
- Key teaching points: Velocity examples may include faster release/update cadence and feedback loops; adaptability examples may include subscription revenue, service model, customer account relationship, and new operating assumptions.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Two-column table: Velocity evidence vs. Adaptability evidence.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Avoid assigning a final quadrant; let students make the judgment.
- Connection to assignment: Demonstrates how to apply, not define, the framework.

## Slide 9: Where Strategy Becomes Visible

- Purpose: Connect strategy to digital touchpoints and operating surfaces.
- Key teaching points: Strategy can show up in subscription/pricing page, Creative Cloud plan/bundle, account relationship, cloud collaboration, product update cadence, usage/retention measurement.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Customer journey strip with visible touchpoints and backstage signals.
- Optional instructor narration notes: The video should teach surfaces as examples of where strategy becomes visible, but Module 2 does not require students to inspect a live Adobe website or app.
- Connection to assignment: Students identify one touchpoint or operating implication.

## Slide 10: How to Use the Framework in the Discussion

- Purpose: Translate lecture into response structure.
- Key teaching points: Start with the strategic problem; apply velocity/adaptability; challenge one traditional assumption; connect to one touchpoint; cite evidence.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Response checklist.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Warn against writing a generic Adobe summary.
- Connection to assignment: Mirrors the suggested response format.

## Slide 11: Evidence and AI/Source Transparency

- Purpose: Clarify evidence discipline.
- Key teaching points: Observed means sourced facts or assigned materials; inferred means reasonable operating implications; judged/recommended means the team's strategic evaluation. AI output is not evidence of current Adobe performance or capabilities.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Three evidence labels stacked as Observed, Inferred, Judged.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Remind students to include source/date for current public data.
- Connection to assignment: Supports rubric criteria 3 and 4.

## Slide 12: Assignment Checklist

- Purpose: Close with actionable completion guidance.
- Key teaching points: Explain Adobe's old problem; apply Kinetic Strategy; distinguish velocity and adaptability; challenge one traditional assumption; identify one touchpoint; cite at least one source; disclose AI/source use.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Checkboxes.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Keep the discussion focused and concise.
- Connection to assignment: Final student-facing checklist before submission.
