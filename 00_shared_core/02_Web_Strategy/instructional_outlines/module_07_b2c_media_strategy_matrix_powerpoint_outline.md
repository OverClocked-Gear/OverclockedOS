# Module 7 Instructional PowerPoint Outline: B2C Digital Consumer Behavior and the Media Strategy Matrix

Purpose: Create an instructor-facing outline for a short explanatory PowerPoint/video that introduces the Module 7 B2C assignment and prepares students to use the Media Strategy Matrix with consumer behavior logic.

## Slide 1: Module 7 Focus

- Purpose: Introduce the B2C consumer-behavior lens.
- Key teaching points: Digital consumers move through nonlinear journeys; awareness and intent shape what they need; Module 7 focuses on consumer behavior rather than disruption.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Consumer journey loop with awareness and intent overlays.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Mention that Spotify is used because it makes personalization, discovery, subscription choice, and retention visible.
- Connection to assignment: Sets up the B2C matrix analysis.

## Slide 2: Media Strategy Matrix Overview

- Purpose: Explain the shared framework.
- Key teaching points: The matrix uses brand awareness and purchase/subscription intent; each quadrant implies a different strategic task; students analyze one quadrant deeply.
- Suggested visual or diagram: 2x2 matrix with awareness and intent axes.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Keep the matrix practical: what does this audience need next?
- Connection to assignment: Required framework.

## Slide 3: The Four B2C Audience States

- Purpose: Translate quadrants into consumer behavior.
- Key teaching points: Low/low needs discovery; low awareness/high intent needs proof and clarity; high awareness/low intent needs relevance or trigger; high/high needs conversion or retention support.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Four quadrant cards with consumer questions.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Avoid overloading students with channel lists.
- Connection to assignment: Helps students choose one quadrant.

## Slide 4: Why Spotify Fits Module 7

- Purpose: Explain the brand choice.
- Key teaching points: Spotify supports discussion of personalization, discovery, subscription choice, playlist/recommendation experience, and lifecycle/retention.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Consumer touchpoint map: discovery, playlist, plan, message, retention.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Do not make unsupported current claims about Spotify features or performance.
- Connection to assignment: Establishes the B2C context.

## Slide 5: Touchpoint Role

- Purpose: Show how strategy appears in public consumer touchpoints.
- Key teaching points: A touchpoint can build awareness, reduce uncertainty, encourage trial, reinforce value, or support retention; students should select one.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Touchpoint-to-job matching table.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Students do not need login or private app access.
- Connection to assignment: Required touchpoint analysis.

## Slide 6: Message and Content Need

- Purpose: Help students move from quadrant to communication decision.
- Key teaching points: Low awareness needs orientation; high intent needs comparison/proof; low intent needs relevance; retention needs value reminders.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Audience state -> message need -> content example.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Stress consumer need, not generic brand promotion.
- Connection to assignment: Required message/content need.

## Slide 7: Friction in Consumer Journeys

- Purpose: Teach students to diagnose what blocks progress.
- Key teaching points: Friction may include unclear value, weak proof, too many choices, subscription uncertainty, trust concerns, or weak relevance.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Journey path with friction markers.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Friction can be inferred, but it must be labeled as inference.
- Connection to assignment: Required friction diagnosis.

## Slide 8: Recommendation and Metric Fit

- Purpose: Connect diagnosis to action and measurement.
- Key teaching points: Recommendations should be specific; metrics should match the behavior the recommendation aims to change; proposed metrics do not need proprietary access.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Recommendation -> behavior -> metric chain.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Discourage vague metrics like "engagement" unless defined.
- Connection to assignment: Required recommendation and metric.

## Slide 9: Evidence Labels

- Purpose: Prevent overclaiming.
- Key teaching points: Observed means visible or course-stated; Inferred means reasonable conclusion; Recommended means the team action; AI output is not company evidence.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Three-column evidence label table.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Use this slide to model careful language without giving an answer.
- Connection to assignment: Required evidence discipline.

## Slide 10: How Module 7 Connects to Module 8

- Purpose: Prepare the paired sequence.
- Key teaching points: Same matrix; different audience logic; B2C emphasizes individual consumer motivation and lifecycle; B2B emphasizes committee decisions, proof, and lead nurture.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Side-by-side B2C/B2B comparison.
- Optional instructor narration notes: This is the bridge to Salesforce in Module 8.
- Connection to assignment: Explains why the same framework appears again.

## Slide 11: Assignment Checklist

- Purpose: Give students a final process guide.
- Key teaching points: Choose one quadrant; select one public touchpoint; diagnose behavior and friction; recommend one improvement; match one metric; disclose sources and AI.
- Suggested visual or diagram: Checklist.
- Optional instructor narration notes: Keep the expected response concise.
- Connection to assignment: Student completion support.
