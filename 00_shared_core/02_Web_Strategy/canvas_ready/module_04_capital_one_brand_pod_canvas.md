# Module 4 Discussion: Capital One — Infrastructure, Trust, and Banking’s Next Horizon

## Purpose

This discussion asks your two-person team to examine one public Capital One digital surface and connect what customers can see to the infrastructure capabilities that likely matter behind the scenes. The focus is customer trust, identity continuity, platform readiness, and infrastructure-enabled service quality.

This is one team discussion submission. There is no Team B assignment, no paired-team reply, and no Brand Pod synthesis.

## Required Frameworks

Use all three:

1. Technology–Organization–Environment Framework, or TOE Framework
2. Integrated Tech Stack — five layers
3. Digital Horizon Planning / Three Horizons

Use TOE to explain technology, organization, and environment factors. Use the Integrated Tech Stack to connect the visible surface to likely capability layers. Use Three Horizons to separate near-term improvement from future capability monitoring.

## Role Lens

Use this combined lens:

- Digital Product & Experience
- CRM, Lifecycle & Loyalty
- Digital Strategy / General Manager

Think like the team responsible for connecting customer trust, identity continuity, relationship quality, operating tradeoffs, and strategic sequencing. Platform/Technology and Analytics are supporting dependencies you may infer, not assigned role lenses.

## LLM Support Step

Use an LLM as a technology translator and hypothesis generator. The LLM may help you understand infrastructure terms, map possible dependencies, and review your draft for overclaiming. It is not evidence.

Suggested prompts:

1. Explain the technology in plain language: Ask the LLM to explain tokenized deposits, stablecoins, programmable finance, identity continuity, or platform readiness in plain language. Tell it not to make claims about Capital One’s current products or capabilities.
2. Map infrastructure capabilities: Ask the LLM to map your public-surface observation to TOE, the Integrated Tech Stack, and Three Horizons. Tell it to label each idea as a hypothesis, not a fact.
3. Review for overreach: Ask the LLM to identify unsupported claims, overreach, or statements that sound like you know Capital One’s internal systems or current crypto/tokenization capabilities.

## Prompt

Examine one public Capital One surface assigned or approved by your instructor. You may use crypto, stablecoins, tokenized deposits, tokenized assets, and programmable finance only as future scenario drivers about trust, ownership, settlement, identity, compliance, and programmability.

Do not claim that Capital One currently offers crypto, stablecoin, tokenized deposit, tokenized asset, wallet, custody, blockchain, AI, or tokenization products or capabilities. Do not give crypto investment advice.

## Access Rules

Use public surfaces only. Do not log in, create an account, begin or submit an application, enter personal or financial information, contact support, test security controls, download apps, or use private account information.

## Required Response Structure

Submit approximately 300-400 words using this structure:

1. Surface examined and access date
2. Customer job or trust issue
3. Observed
4. Inferred
5. Horizon analysis:
   - Horizon 1: optimize today’s digital banking experience
   - Horizon 2: extend platform readiness
   - Horizon 3: future capability to monitor but not rush
6. Recommendation: one Horizon 1 or Horizon 2 operating improvement
7. Metric and why it fits
8. Sources and AI disclosure

## Evidence Labels

- Observed: directly visible on the public surface or assigned course material
- Inferred: a reasonable infrastructure, data, identity, process, compliance, measurement, or operating dependency
- Recommended: your team’s proposed Horizon 1 or Horizon 2 operating improvement

Do not claim to know Capital One’s internal systems, vendors, databases, algorithms, security controls, analytics, compliance operations, internal metrics, AI capabilities, blockchain capabilities, crypto offerings, or tokenization capabilities.

## How the Work Will Be Evaluated

Your work will be evaluated using the four course discussion criteria:

1. Strategic Diagnosis and Critical Reasoning
2. Application of Course Frameworks with Specific Examples
3. Use of Examples and Evidence
4. Writing Clarity, Organization, and Proper Citation of Course Concepts

Your surface observation, framework application, horizon analysis, recommendation, metric, sources, and AI disclosure provide evidence within those four criteria.

## AI and Source Transparency

Disclose:

- AI tool used
- prompt or concise prompt summary

- what your team verified, corrected, rejected, or rewrote

AI output is not evidence that a current Capital One feature, system, metric, product, or capability exists. Your team remains responsible for direct observation, course-framework application, source accuracy, and majority authorship.
