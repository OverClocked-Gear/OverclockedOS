## Module Objective Alignment

Syllabus objectives:

- Design customer-centric innovation processes that balance creativity with systematic execution.
- Design agile marketing operations that balance flexibility with strategic consistency.
- Develop data-driven optimization approaches for continuous improvement.

The Glossier case supports these objectives through community-led brand building, customer feedback loops, product/experience innovation, content and social/community surfaces, omnichannel expansion and/or distribution tradeoffs where supported by the case, agile experimentation, data-driven optimization, and stakeholder trust.

## Case Rationale

Glossier is a strong Module 13 case because:

- It gives students a beauty / consumer brand example of community-led innovation.
- It connects customer conversation, product development, content/community, channel choices, and growth strategy.
- It surfaces the tension between brand intimacy and scaling.
- It allows students to evaluate how an agile marketing/innovation system should evolve as a digitally native brand matures.
- It complements Domino’s and Kraft Heinz rather than duplicating them.

## Case Scenario

Glossier built momentum by treating customers and community as part of the brand-building and innovation system, not merely as downstream buyers. Its challenge is how to preserve community trust, customer intimacy, product relevance, and brand distinctiveness while scaling across channels, retail/distribution options, content/community surfaces, and performance expectations.

The case asks students to advise Glossier on how to build a repeatable innovation and agile marketing system that connects customer insight, product decisions, content/community engagement, channel strategy, measurement, and experimentation.

Do not claim current Glossier features, channel relationships, retail presence, social platforms, metrics, or performance unless they are in the assigned HBR case, course materials, or instructor-verified sources.

## Framework Contract

Required synthesis framework:

- Innovation Full Flywheel / Reinvention Flywheel

Use the exact canonical framework name from the Overclocked framework appendix if available. Appendix B lists the canonical Chapter 15 framework as Reinvention Flywheel.

Students should use the flywheel to explain how Glossier can turn customer insight into product/experience decisions, test those decisions, activate them through content/community/channel surfaces, learn from performance, and reinvest learning into the next cycle.

Required supporting framework:

- Agile Marketing Operating System

Students should use this to evaluate how Glossier should organize work, test ideas, learn from data, and maintain strategic consistency while moving quickly.

Choose one additional supporting framework:

1. Insight Value Chain
2. Consumer Journey Loop
3. Business Model Evolution Framework

## Case Assignment Question

How should Glossier evolve its community-led innovation and agile marketing system so it can keep scaling without losing customer trust, brand distinctiveness, or product relevance?

## Required Student Tasks

Students should:

1. Diagnose the central strategic problem or opportunity Glossier faces.
2. Apply the Innovation Full Flywheel / Reinvention Flywheel as the required synthesis framework.
3. Apply the Agile Marketing Operating System.
4. Choose one additional supporting framework from the approved list.
5. Identify two or three stakeholder groups whose needs or incentives must be balanced.
6. Analyze two or three surfaces/touchpoints where Glossier’s innovation and customer relationship become visible.
7. Identify one risk or tension created by scaling a community-led brand.
8. Recommend the top three priorities Glossier should pursue.
9. Explain how the priorities work together as an innovation and agile marketing system.
10. Propose two or three metrics that would show whether the recommendation is working.
11. Include case/course citations and AI/source transparency.

## Stakeholder Lens

Use case-aligned stakeholder framing, not a Brand Pod.

Primary integration lens:

- Digital Strategy / General Manager

Required stakeholder groups to consider:

- Customers / community members
- Glossier leadership / brand strategy team
- Product / innovation team
- Content, community, and CRM/lifecycle teams
- Analytics & Experimentation team
- Channel/retail/distribution partners, only where supported by the case or instructor-verified materials

Optional stakeholders:

- Investors / financial stakeholders
- Employees / internal operating teams
- Creators / influencers / advocates, only if supported by the case or instructor-provided materials

Students should not try to write a full stakeholder analysis for every group. They should identify the two or three stakeholder tensions most important to their recommendation.

## Surfaces and Touchpoints

Relevant surfaces/touchpoints may include:

- Glossier.com or public product/detail pages
- public review, rating, or user-generated-content surfaces
- Into The Gloss or other assigned content/community materials, if included in the case packet
- social/community content referenced in the HBR case
- email/SMS/lifecycle messaging as a conceptual touchpoint
- physical retail or wholesale/retail discovery surfaces if discussed in the case or instructor-verified materials
- product launch / drop / feedback-loop surfaces
- public brand/content/community pages
- customer service or feedback channels discussed in the case

Do not require students to log in, create an account, purchase, submit personal information, subscribe, download an app, or inspect private customer experiences.

If current public surfaces are used, students must cite source/date and label them separately from HBR case facts.

## Evidence Discipline

Require students to distinguish:

- Observed: facts from the HBR Glossier case, assigned course materials, or instructor-approved public evidence
- Inferred: reasonable conclusions about customers, community, product learning, agile marketing, channel tradeoffs, or stakeholder tensions
- Recommended: the team’s proposed strategic priorities

Students must not claim to know Glossier’s current internal strategy, customer data, social-commerce performance, retail economics, inventory strategy, channel economics, algorithmic personalization, CRM segmentation, or internal decision-making unless provided in assigned materials.

AI output may help brainstorm, organize, or pressure-test the recommendation, but it is not evidence of current Glossier capabilities, strategy, metrics, or performance.

## Strong-Answer Indicators

- Identifies one central strategic problem or opportunity.
- Does not simply retell the Glossier case.
- Uses the Innovation Full Flywheel / Reinvention Flywheel as an operating system, not a buzzword.
- Applies Agile Marketing Operating System to explain experimentation, iteration, cadence, and strategic consistency.
- Balances customer/community needs with business growth and channel scale.
- Analyzes specific surfaces/touchpoints where customer relationship and learning become visible.
- Identifies a realistic scaling tension, such as community authenticity vs. growth, channel expansion vs. brand control, product speed vs. quality, or personalization vs. trust.
- Prioritizes top three actions.
- Proposes metrics tied to learning, customer relationship, product adoption, and business outcomes.
- Cites HBR case/course evidence.
- Discloses AI/source use transparently.

## Weak-Answer Indicators

- Summarizes Glossier without diagnosing the strategic problem.
- Treats community as only social media followers.
- Assumes customer feedback automatically creates good innovation.
- Recommends generic “do more TikTok” or “launch more products.”
- Over-focuses on tone/slang rather than strategy.
- Uses AI-generated ideas as evidence.
- Makes unsupported claims about current Glossier channels, retail, product metrics, or customer data.
- Ignores stakeholder tradeoffs.
- Lists many frameworks without applying them.
- Lacks metrics or executive conclusion.

## Metrics Guidance

Suggest metric families, not model answers.

- product concept validation rate

- test-to-launch conversion rate
- repeat purchase
- retention
- customer feedback quality or volume
- community participation quality
- content-to-product insight conversion
- launch sell-through
- product review quality
- NPS or sentiment trend, if available in assigned materials
- CRM engagement
- cohort repeat behavior
- experiment learning velocity
- channel contribution to acquisition or retention
- customer lifetime value, if justified

Students should select two or three metrics that fit their recommendation.
