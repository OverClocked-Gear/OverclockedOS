## Module Objective Alignment

Syllabus objectives:

- Design comprehensive go-to-market strategies that integrate digital and traditional channels.
- Create adaptive processes that enable continuous strategy refinement.
- Evaluate different organizational structures for effective GTM execution.

The Kraft Heinz case supports these objectives through Agile@Scale, cross-functional pods, faster product development cycles, insight-to-launch loops, integrated retail, marketing, commercialization, and feedback systems, and adaptive GTM execution in a mature CPG company. The case asks students to evaluate not simply whether Kraft Heinz can move faster, but whether it can build a repeatable GTM operating system that learns, prioritizes, and scales responsibly.

- The case makes agile GTM concrete through product development timelines, cross-functional pods, retail launch coordination, customer insight, and speed-to-market.
- Kraft Heinz better fits the Module 11 objectives around GTM execution, adaptive processes, and organizational structure.

Do not overstate or invent current Kraft Heinz claims. Use only course-supported claims and flag anything needing instructor verification.

## Case Scenario

Kraft Heinz is a mature CPG company operating in categories where consumer tastes, retail dynamics, cultural trends, and competitive pressure move faster than traditional product-development and marketing cycles. Legacy stage-gate planning, functional silos, and long commercialization timelines can cause brands to miss cultural moments and shelf opportunities.

To increase GTM velocity, Kraft Heinz implemented Agile@Scale and cross-functional pods that brought marketing, R&D, commercialization, analytics, and launch planning closer together. The strategic question is whether this model gives Kraft Heinz a repeatable growth system or merely a faster way to launch individual products.

Students advise Kraft Heinz on how to sustain GTM velocity, protect strategic discipline, and scale agile marketing without creating chaos.

## Case Assignment Question

How should Kraft Heinz use Agile@Scale, cross-functional pods, insight-driven product development, and integrated GTM orchestration to sustain CPG relevance in a faster-moving consumer market?

## Required Student Tasks

Students should:

1. Diagnose the central GTM problem or opportunity Kraft Heinz faces.
2. Apply the Agile Marketing Operating System to assess the operating model.
3. Apply the Kinetic Strategy Framework to evaluate velocity and adaptability.
4. Choose one additional supporting framework from the approved list.
5. Explain how cross-functional pods change the insight-to-launch process.
6. Identify one tension or risk created by agile GTM at scale.
7. Recommend the top three priorities Kraft Heinz should pursue to sustain GTM velocity without losing discipline.
8. Explain how the priorities work together as an operating system rather than disconnected tactics.
9. Propose two or three metrics that would show whether Agile@Scale and GTM orchestration are working.
10. Include course/source citations and AI/source transparency.

## Strong-Answer Indicators

- Identifies one central GTM problem or opportunity.
- Explains why traditional CPG planning cycles are too slow for current market conditions.
- Applies Agile Marketing Operating System as an operating model, not a buzzword.
- Uses Kinetic Strategy to distinguish speed from adaptability.
- Explains how pods reduce handoffs and improve learning cycles.
- Connects consumer insight to product development, retail activation, media, measurement, and feedback.
- Identifies a realistic risk of agile at scale, such as fragmentation, governance gaps, brand inconsistency, or speed without learning.
- Prioritizes top three actions.
- Proposes metrics tied to GTM velocity and market learning.
- Cites case/course evidence.
- Discloses AI/source use transparently.

## Weak-Answer Indicators

- Summarizes Kraft Heinz without diagnosing the GTM problem.
- Treats agile as simply “moving faster.”
- Ignores adaptability or learning quality.
- Lists many tactics without prioritization.
- Makes unsupported claims about current Kraft Heinz metrics, team counts, AI capabilities, or internal systems.
- Confuses Kraft Heinz with Heinz Marketing.
- Recommends generic “use more AI” or “make more social content” without operating logic.
- Lacks metrics or conclusion.
- Uses AI output as evidence.
