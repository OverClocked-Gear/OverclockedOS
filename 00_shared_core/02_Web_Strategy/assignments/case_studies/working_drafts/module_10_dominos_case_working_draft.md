## Module Objective Alignment

Syllabus objectives:

- Understand the relationship between owned media, content strategy, and customer empowerment.
- Create content strategies specifically designed to earn amplification.
- Develop an integrated paid media strategy that aligns with business objectives and connects effectively with content and earned media.

The Domino’s case supports these objectives by asking students to evaluate how owned channels, paid media, earned amplification, direct customer relationships, digital ordering, loyalty, data, and marketplace/platform pressure interact. The case moves beyond channel definitions and asks students to advise Domino’s on how media, customer journey, and business model choices work together.

## Case Scenario

Domino’s has long been recognized for digital ordering, delivery convenience, and using digital channels to reduce friction in the pizza-ordering journey. But the food-delivery market is increasingly platform-mediated. Third-party delivery apps, aggregators, changing media behavior, and rising customer expectations create pressure on Domino’s to defend its owned customer relationship while continuing to acquire, retain, and serve customers across paid, earned, and owned channels.

The case asks students to advise Domino’s on how to strengthen its digital media ecosystem and direct customer relationship while avoiding overdependence on external platforms.

Do not claim current Domino’s features or metrics unless they are in the assigned case or course materials.

## Framework Contract

Required synthesis framework:

- Business Model Evolution Framework

Students should use this to explain how Domino’s digital ecosystem supports or changes the business model, especially around owned customer relationships, ordering behavior, loyalty, data, delivery/channel control, and value capture.

Required supporting framework choice:

Students must choose two of the following:

1. Owned / Earned / Paid Media Ecosystem
2. Consumer Journey Loop
3. Insight Value Chain
4. GTM Orchestration Framework
5. Content → Trust → Value Model, only if content or earned amplification is central to the recommendation

Optional:

- Kinetic Strategy Framework, only if used briefly to discuss adaptability and velocity in responding to platform pressure.

Do not force:

- every media framework
- full business model canvas
- full customer journey map
- speculative data monetization claims
- technical infrastructure stack analysis
- current digital feature claims not in assigned materials
- unsupported claims about third-party platform partnerships or performance

Strong case work applies a small number of frameworks deeply to make an executive recommendation.

## Case Assignment Question

How should Domino’s evolve its digital media ecosystem, owned channels, and customer journey to defend and extend its direct customer relationship in a platform-mediated food-delivery market?

## Required Student Tasks

Students should:

1. Diagnose the central strategic problem or opportunity.
2. Apply the Business Model Evolution Framework.
3. Choose two supporting frameworks from the approved list.
4. Analyze Domino’s owned, earned, and paid media ecosystem.
5. Identify where the customer journey creates or loses value.
6. Identify one platform-pressure or disintermediation risk.
7. Recommend the top three priorities Domino’s should pursue.
8. Explain how the priorities work together rather than as disconnected tactics.
9. Propose two or three metrics that would show whether the recommendation is working.
10. Include course/source citations and AI/source transparency.

## Evidence Discipline

Require students to distinguish:

- Observed: facts in the assigned Domino’s case, course materials, or public digital evidence if instructor allows it
- Inferred: reasonable conclusions about customer journey, media ecosystem, data, loyalty, business model, or platform pressure
- Recommended: the team’s proposed strategic priorities

Students must not claim to know Domino’s internal data, campaign performance, loyalty segmentation, customer lists, third-party contract terms, media buying, targeting, attribution, or internal systems.

AI output may help brainstorm, organize, or pressure-test the recommendation, but it is not evidence of current Domino’s capabilities or performance.

## Strong-Answer Indicators

- Identifies one central strategic problem or opportunity.
- Does not answer as a list of disconnected tactics.
- Uses Business Model Evolution Framework to explain value creation and value capture.
- Selects two supporting frameworks that fit the argument.
- Connects owned, earned, and paid media to the customer journey.
- Explains why direct customer relationships matter.
- Identifies a realistic platform-pressure or disintermediation risk.
- Prioritizes top three actions.
- Proposes metrics tied to the recommendation.
- Cites case/course evidence.
- Discloses AI/source use transparently.

## Weak-Answer Indicators

- Summarizes Domino’s without strategic diagnosis.
- Lists many tactics without prioritization.
- Treats third-party delivery platforms only as distribution rather than strategic pressure.
- Uses media terminology without connecting to customer journey or business model.
- Makes unsupported data or partnership claims.
- Overclaims what Domino’s currently knows about customers.
- Recommends generic “more social media” or “more app features.”
- Uses AI output as evidence.
- Lacks metrics or conclusion.
