# Module 10 Case Study: Domino’s — Digital Media Ecosystem and Owned Customer Relationships

## Purpose

This group case asks you to evaluate how Domino’s can use its digital media ecosystem, owned channels, customer journey, and business model to defend and extend its direct customer relationship.

This is a group case assignment, not a discussion post. Do not write peer replies.

## Scenario

Domino’s has long been associated with digital ordering, delivery convenience, and reducing friction in the pizza-ordering journey. The food-delivery market is now increasingly shaped by third-party delivery apps, aggregators, changing media behavior, and rising customer expectations.

Your group should advise Domino’s on how to strengthen its digital media ecosystem and direct customer relationship while avoiding overdependence on external platforms.

Do not claim current Domino’s features, offers, partnerships, metrics, customer data, or internal strategy unless they appear in the assigned case, course materials, or an instructor-approved source.

## Required Framework

- Business Model Evolution Framework

Use this framework to explain how Domino’s digital ecosystem supports or changes value creation and value capture, especially around owned customer relationships, ordering behavior, loyalty, data, delivery/channel control, and platform pressure.

## Choose Two Supporting Frameworks

Choose the two supporting frameworks that best strengthen your recommendation. Do not try to use every framework.

1. Owned / Earned / Paid Media Ecosystem
2. Consumer Journey Loop
3. Insight Value Chain
4. GTM Orchestration Framework
5. Content → Trust → Value Model, if content or earned amplification is central to the recommendation

## Case Prompt

Your group should:

1. Diagnose Domino’s central strategic problem or opportunity.
2. Apply the Business Model Evolution Framework.
3. Apply two supporting frameworks.
4. Analyze how owned, earned, and paid media connect to the customer journey.
5. Identify one platform-pressure or disintermediation risk.
6. Recommend the top three strategic priorities Domino’s should pursue.
7. Explain how the priorities work together.
8. Propose two or three metrics.
9. Close with a clear executive conclusion.
10. Include sources and AI disclosure.

Do not log in, create an account, order food, enter payment information, contact support, download an app, or use private account access.

## Evidence Expectations

- Observed: facts in the assigned Domino’s case, course materials, or approved public evidence.
- Inferred: your reasonable conclusions about customer journey, media ecosystem, data, loyalty, business model, or platform pressure.
- Recommended: your group’s proposed strategic priorities.

Use assigned case facts, course concepts, and approved sources. Do not invent current Domino’s data, campaign performance, loyalty segmentation, customer data, third-party contract terms, or internal strategy.
