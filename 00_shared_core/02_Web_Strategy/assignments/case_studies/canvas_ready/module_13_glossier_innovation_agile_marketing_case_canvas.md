# Module 13 Case Study: Glossier — Community, Innovation, and Agile Brand Reinvention

## Purpose

This group case asks you to evaluate how Glossier can evolve its community-led innovation and agile marketing system while preserving customer trust, brand distinctiveness, and product relevance.

## Scenario

Glossier built its brand by connecting closely with customers and community. The strategic challenge is how to keep that customer intimacy and feedback loop alive while scaling product, channel, content, and marketing operations.

Your group is advising Glossier on how to build a repeatable innovation and agile marketing system.

## Required Frameworks

1. Innovation Full Flywheel / Reinvention Flywheel
2. Agile Marketing Operating System

Use the exact framework names provided in class or the assigned Overclocked materials.

## Choose One Supporting Framework

Choose the supporting framework that best strengthens your recommendation. Do not try to use every framework.

1. Insight Value Chain
2. Consumer Journey Loop
3. Business Model Evolution Framework
4. Content → Trust → Value Model
5. Kinetic Strategy Framework, only if useful for adaptability and velocity
6. Impact × Feasibility Prioritization, if approved by instructor

## Case Prompt

Your group should:

1. Diagnose Glossier’s central strategic problem or opportunity.
2. Apply the Innovation Full Flywheel / Reinvention Flywheel.
3. Apply the Agile Marketing Operating System.
4. Apply one supporting framework.
5. Identify two or three stakeholder groups Glossier must balance.
6. Analyze two or three surfaces/touchpoints where customer relationship, community, product feedback, or brand experience become visible.
7. Identify one scaling risk or tension.
8. Recommend the top three priorities Glossier should pursue.
9. Explain how the priorities work together.
10. Propose two or three metrics.
11. Close with a clear executive conclusion.
12. Include sources and AI disclosure.

## Stakeholder Lens

Consider two or three of the following:

- Customers / community members
- Glossier leadership / brand strategy team
- Product / innovation team
- Content, community, and CRM/lifecycle teams
- Analytics & Experimentation team
- Channel/retail/distribution partners, if supported by the case or instructor-approved material

## Surfaces / Touchpoints

You may reference surfaces or touchpoints from the HBR case, assigned course materials, or instructor-approved public evidence.

Examples may include:

- product/detail pages
- reviews or user-generated content
- content/community materials
- social/community surfaces described in the case
- lifecycle messaging as a conceptual touchpoint
- product launch or feedback-loop surfaces
- retail/distribution surfaces if supported by the case or instructor-approved materials

Do not log in, create an account, purchase, subscribe, download an app, or inspect private customer experiences.

## Evidence Expectations

- Observed: facts from the HBR Glossier case, course materials, or approved sources.
- Inferred: your reasonable conclusions about customers, community, product learning, agile marketing, channel tradeoffs, or stakeholder tensions.
- Recommended: your group’s proposed strategic priorities.

Use HBR case facts, course concepts, and approved sources. Do not invent current Glossier data, product metrics, retail/channel details, social commerce capabilities, customer segmentation, internal strategy, or performance.

## How the Work Will Be Evaluated

Your work will be evaluated using only the four canonical criteria:

1. Strategic Diagnosis and Critical Reasoning
2. Application of Course Frameworks with Specific Examples
3. Use of Examples and Evidence
4. Writing Clarity, Organization, and Proper Citation of Course Concepts

Your diagnosis, framework application, case evidence, stakeholder/touchpoint analysis, recommendation, metrics, and conclusion provide evidence within those criteria. They are not separate rubric categories.

## AI and Source Transparency

If your group uses AI, disclose the tool used, the purpose of use, and what your group checked, corrected, rejected, or rewrote. Include a prompt or concise prompt summary if your instructor requires it.

The group should write the vast majority of the submission. AI output may help brainstorm or organize, but it is not evidence of current Glossier capabilities, strategy, metrics, community health, social commerce, channel performance, or product results.
