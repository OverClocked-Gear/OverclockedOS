# Module 11 Case Study: Kraft Heinz — Agile@Scale and GTM Velocity

## Purpose

This group case asks you to evaluate how Kraft Heinz can use Agile@Scale, cross-functional pods, insight-driven product development, and integrated GTM orchestration to move faster without losing strategic discipline.

## Scenario

Kraft Heinz is a mature CPG company operating in categories where consumer tastes, retail dynamics, cultural trends, and competitive pressure can move faster than traditional product-development and marketing cycles. Legacy stage-gate planning, functional silos, and long commercialization timelines can make it harder for brands to respond to cultural moments, shopper needs, and shelf opportunities.

This is a CPG agile GTM case. It is not a general AI case, not a media-plan assignment, and not a B2B consulting methodology case.

Your group should advise Kraft Heinz on how to sustain GTM velocity, protect strategic discipline, and scale agile marketing without creating chaos.

## Required Frameworks

1. Agile Marketing Operating System
2. Kinetic Strategy Framework

Use Agile Marketing Operating System to evaluate how Kraft Heinz organizes and executes GTM work. Use Kinetic Strategy to evaluate whether Kraft Heinz is improving velocity, adaptability, or both.

## Choose One Supporting Framework

Choose the supporting framework that best strengthens your recommendation. Do not try to use every framework.

1. GTM Orchestration Framework
2. Insight Value Chain
3. Digital Horizon Planning
4. Business Model Evolution Framework

## Case Prompt

Your group should:

1. Diagnose Kraft Heinz’s central GTM problem or opportunity.
2. Apply the Agile Marketing Operating System.
3. Apply the Kinetic Strategy Framework.
4. Apply one supporting framework.
5. Explain how cross-functional pods change the insight-to-launch process.
6. Identify one risk or tension created by agile GTM at scale.
7. Recommend the top three priorities Kraft Heinz should pursue.
8. Explain how the priorities work together.
9. Propose two or three metrics.
10. Close with a clear executive conclusion.
11. Include sources and AI disclosure.

## Evidence Expectations

- Observed: facts in assigned case materials, course materials, or approved sources.
- Inferred: your reasonable conclusions about agile pods, GTM velocity, innovation operating model, market learning, or CPG execution.
- Recommended: your group’s proposed strategic priorities.

Use assigned case facts, course concepts, and approved sources. Do not invent current Kraft Heinz data, pod counts, AI capabilities, launch timelines, ROI, campaign performance, product metrics, or internal strategy.
