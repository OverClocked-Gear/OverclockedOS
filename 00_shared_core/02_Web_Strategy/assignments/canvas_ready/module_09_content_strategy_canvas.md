# Module 9 Discussion: Content Strategy — From Content to Trust to Business Value

## Purpose

This assignment asks you to evaluate how content creates value beyond attention. You will compare Red Bull and Mailchimp to see how content can educate, entertain, inspire, build trust, strengthen brand equity, and support business outcomes.

Content strategy is not simply "making engaging content." It is a system for serving an audience need, building trust, and creating measurable value.

## Required Framework

- Content → Trust → Value Model

Use the model to show how content creates value for the audience, how that value builds trust, and how trust can support a business outcome.

## Prompt

1. Select one public content asset or content hub from Red Bull and one from Mailchimp.
2. Apply the Content → Trust → Value Model to each brand.
3. Identify the audience need each content asset addresses.
4. Explain what type of value the content provides.
5. Explain how the content could build trust or brand equity.
6. Identify one business outcome each brand's content could support.
7. Compare which brand has the stronger content system and why.
8. Recommend one improvement for one of the two brands.
9. Propose one business-linked metric and explain why it fits.
10. Cite at least one source or assigned course example. Include source/date for current public assets.
11. Include AI/source transparency.

## Role Lens

Use:

- Digital Merchandising & Content
- Analytics & Experimentation

Think like the team responsible for deciding what content should exist, what audience need it serves, and how the company would know whether it is creating business value.

## Digital Touchpoint Cue

Inspect one public content asset or content hub per brand.

Examples:

- Red Bull Media House or a public Red Bull story, event, video, or content hub
- Mailchimp Presents or a public Mailchimp educational, storytelling, or small-business content hub

Do not log in, subscribe, sign up, download an app, or enter personal information.

## Evidence Labels

- Observed: content asset, message, format, audience cue, call to action, source/date, or assigned course fact.
- Inferred: your reasonable conclusion about audience need, trust-building, brand equity, or business purpose.
- Recommended: your proposed content improvement or metric.
