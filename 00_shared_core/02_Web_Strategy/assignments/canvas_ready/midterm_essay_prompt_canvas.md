# Midterm Essay: Connected Fitness at a Strategic Crossroads

## Purpose

This essay asks you to connect Chapters 1-5 concepts to diagnose a strategic problem or opportunity and recommend a path forward. The goal is synthesis, not breadth. Use a small number of frameworks well rather than trying to mention every course concept.

## Scenario

PulseFit is a fictional connected-fitness company that built early growth around premium home exercise equipment, a subscription content app, and a community layer where members join challenges, follow instructors, and share progress.

Growth is now slowing. Premium hardware is becoming easier to copy, and customers are questioning whether expensive equipment is necessary. Churn is rising among subscribers who no longer use the app consistently. Lower-cost app-only fitness competitors are attracting customers who want flexibility without buying hardware. Wearable ecosystems are becoming more central to health and activity tracking. Creator-led fitness alternatives are building loyal audiences outside traditional fitness platforms. Employer wellness programs, insurance partnerships, and health-related partnerships may create new opportunities if PulseFit can execute responsibly.

Leadership is divided. One group wants to defend the hardware-centered model with new equipment and market expansion. Another group wants to reinvent PulseFit as a broader digital fitness and wellness platform built around the app, community, data-enabled personalization, partnerships, and ongoing engagement. A third group argues for a hybrid path that protects the premium hardware business while building a more flexible platform around content, community, wearables, and partnerships.

PulseFit faces finite resources, inconsistent customer insight, infrastructure limitations, slowing engagement, and weak cross-functional alignment. The executive team needs a recommendation about whether to defend the hardware-centered model, reinvent as a digital fitness/wellness platform, or pursue a hybrid path.
