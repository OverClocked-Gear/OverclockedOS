# Module 8 Discussion: B2B Buyer Behavior, Platforms, and the Media Strategy Matrix

## Purpose

This discussion asks you to use the Media Strategy Matrix to analyze a B2B buyer journey. You will use the same framework from Module 7, but the audience logic changes: B2B buyers often involve committees, proof, risk reduction, lead nurture, and platform/ecosystem value.

Recommended brand: Salesforce.

## Required Framework

- Media Strategy Matrix

Use two dimensions:

- Brand awareness: low or high
- Purchase/evaluation intent: low or high

Choose one quadrant and use it to guide your analysis. Do not try to cover every quadrant.

## Prompt

Analyze a Salesforce B2B buyer journey by answering:

1. Which Media Strategy Matrix quadrant are you analyzing?
2. What B2B buyer behavior is likely in that audience state?
3. What does the buying committee likely need?
4. What public Salesforce surface fits this buyer state?
5. What is the content role of that surface?
6. What trust or proof does the buyer need?
7. What lead-nurture path or next step would make sense without submitting a form?
8. What improvement would you recommend?
9. What metric would show whether your recommendation worked, and why?

Do not submit a form, request a demo, contact sales, create an account, log in, make a purchase, or inspect private platform access.

## Role Lens

Use a light role lens:

- Performance Marketing
- CRM, Lifecycle & Loyalty
- Digital Strategy / General Manager
- Platform, Technology & Operations as a dependency only

Think about how a B2B team helps a buyer move from awareness to evaluation, proof, consensus, and a qualified next step.

## Public Surface Options

You may use one public Salesforce surface, such as:

- product page
- industry solution page
- customer story
- research report
- Trailhead or learning content
- demo-request page, without submitting the form
- event or webinar page
- pricing or contact-sales page, without contacting sales

## Evidence Labels

- Observed: stated in course materials or visible on a public surface.
- Inferred: your reasonable conclusion about buying committee needs, proof requirements, nurture logic, or platform/ecosystem implications.
- Recommended: your proposed improvement and metric.

## Suggested Format

1. Media Strategy Matrix quadrant
2. B2B buyer behavior and committee needs
3. Public surface and content role
4. Trust/proof requirement
5. Lead-nurture path or next step
6. Recommendation
7. Metric
8. Sources and AI disclosure

## How the Work Will Be Evaluated

Your work will be evaluated using only the four canonical criteria:

Your quadrant choice, buying committee analysis, public-surface evidence, recommendation, metric, evidence labels, and source/AI transparency provide evidence within those four criteria.

## AI and Source Transparency

If you use AI, disclose the tool, the purpose of use, and what you checked, corrected, rejected, or rewrote. AI output is not evidence of current Salesforce capabilities, campaign strategy, targeting, personalization, analytics, lead scoring, platform performance, or customer outcomes.

Cite any current public claim with source and date.
