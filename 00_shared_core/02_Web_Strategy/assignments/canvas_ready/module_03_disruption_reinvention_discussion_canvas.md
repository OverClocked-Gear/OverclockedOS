# Module 3 Discussion: Digital Disruption and Reinvention — Tesla, Adoption, and Competitive Response

## Purpose

This discussion asks you to evaluate Tesla as an example of digital disruption and then compare how one incumbent or competitor responded. The goal is not to praise or criticize Tesla in general. Your goal is to explain the disruption mechanism, connect it to adoption logic, and make a strategic judgment about competitive response.

## Required Framework

- Five Catalysts of Disruption

Use the catalysts to explain what accelerated or stalled adoption and why Tesla's disruption was strategically important. Focus on which catalyst or catalysts mattered most, not on listing every catalyst.

## Optional Supporting Frameworks

You may also use:

- Diffusion of Innovation
- Crossing the Chasm
- Diffusion–Disruption Grid
- Offensive vs. Defensive Posture
- Kinetic Strategy Framework, only if used briefly to explain competitor adaptability or velocity

Do not use unrelated frameworks as the main answer.

## Prompt

In your team response:

1. Explain the disruptive shift Tesla represented in the automotive market.
2. Apply the Five Catalysts of Disruption to identify which catalyst or catalysts mattered most.
3. Use adoption/chasm logic to explain where adoption accelerated, stalled, or moved toward a broader market.
4. Choose one incumbent or competitor and evaluate whether its response was primarily defensive or offensive.
5. Identify one customer-facing or operating touchpoint where the disruption became visible.
6. Use at least one cited source or assigned reading example. If you use current public data or competitor claims, include source and date.
7. Include AI/source transparency.

## Role Lens

Use the Digital Strategy / General Manager lens.

Think like the leader deciding whether a company should defend its legacy model, adapt its current offering, or build a more disruptive response.

## Digital Touchpoint Cue

You do not need to inspect a live Tesla or competitor website/app.

You may reference one relevant touchpoint or operating implication, such as EV research/configuration, charging/support ecosystem, mobile app ownership experience, connected-vehicle features, direct sales, over-the-air updates, service, or competitor digital evidence.

## Evidence Labels

Use these labels to keep your analysis clear:

- Observed: sourced company facts, assigned case/course facts, or public data
- Inferred: reasonable adoption, competitor, or operating implications based on the evidence
- Judged / Recommended: your team's evaluation of disruption posture and competitor response

Do not invent current data. Cite source and date for current claims about Tesla, competitors, EV sales, charging, software, pricing, adoption, market share, apps, or product capabilities.

## Suggested Format

1. Tesla's disruptive shift
2. Catalyst analysis
3. Adoption/chasm analysis
4. Competitor response: defensive or offensive
5. Digital touchpoint or operating implication
6. Evidence/source
7. AI/source disclosure

## How the Work Will Be Evaluated

Your work will be evaluated using the four course discussion criteria:

1. Strategic Diagnosis and Critical Reasoning
2. Application of Course Frameworks with Specific Examples
3. Use of Examples and Evidence
4. Writing Clarity, Organization, and Proper Citation of Course Concepts

Your catalyst analysis, adoption logic, competitor evaluation, cited evidence, and touchpoint connection all provide evidence within these criteria.

## AI and Source Transparency

If your team uses AI, disclose the tool used, the purpose of use, and what you checked, corrected, rejected, or rewrote. AI output is not evidence of current company performance, current product capability, current market data, or current competitor posture. Your team is responsible for verifying current claims with cited sources and for writing the majority of the final submission.
