# Module 7 Discussion: B2C Digital Consumer Behavior and the Media Strategy Matrix

## Purpose

This discussion asks you to use the Media Strategy Matrix to analyze a B2C consumer journey. You will focus on how awareness and purchase/subscription intent change the touchpoint, message, friction, recommendation, and metric.

Recommended brand: Spotify.

## Required Framework

- Media Strategy Matrix

Use two dimensions:

- Brand awareness: low or high
- Purchase/subscription intent: low or high

Choose one quadrant and use it to guide your analysis. Do not try to cover every quadrant.

## Prompt

Analyze a Spotify consumer journey by answering:

1. Which Media Strategy Matrix quadrant are you analyzing?
2. What consumer behavior is likely in that audience state?
3. What public or course-described touchpoint matters for this audience?
4. What message or content does the consumer need at that point?
5. What friction might prevent the consumer from moving forward?
6. What improvement would you recommend?
7. What metric would show whether your recommendation worked, and why?

Do not log in, download an app, enter personal information, make a purchase, or inspect a private account experience.

## Role Lens

Use a light role lens:

- Digital Product & Experience
- CRM, Lifecycle & Loyalty
- Performance Marketing as support

Think about how a consumer moves from attention to consideration, trial, subscription, repeat use, or retention.

## Evidence Labels

- Observed: stated in course materials or visible on a public surface.
- Inferred: your reasonable conclusion about consumer behavior, motivation, friction, or lifecycle need.
- Recommended: your proposed improvement and metric.

## Suggested Format

1. Media Strategy Matrix quadrant
2. Consumer behavior diagnosis
3. Touchpoint role
4. Message/content need
5. Likely friction
6. Recommendation
7. Metric
8. Sources and AI disclosure

## How the Work Will Be Evaluated

Your work will be evaluated using only the four canonical criteria:

1. Strategic Diagnosis and Critical Reasoning
2. Application of Course Frameworks with Specific Examples
3. Use of Examples and Evidence
4. Writing Clarity, Organization, and Proper Citation of Course Concepts

Your quadrant choice, touchpoint analysis, recommendation, metric, evidence labels, and source/AI transparency provide evidence within those four criteria.

## AI and Source Transparency

If you use AI, disclose the tool, the purpose of use, and what you checked, corrected, rejected, or rewrote. AI output is not evidence of current Spotify capabilities, campaign strategy, targeting, personalization, analytics, or performance.

Cite any current public claim with source and date.
