# Module 5 Discussion: Deere — From Equipment Manufacturer to Precision Agriculture Platform

## Purpose

This discussion asks your team to evaluate how Deere's business model changes when it moves from selling equipment toward operating a connected precision-agriculture platform. The central question is not only "What does Deere sell?" but "How does Deere create, deliver, and capture value when machines, data, software, workflows, dealers, partners, and farm decisions become connected?"

Treat this as a business model and platform strategy assignment, not a technical architecture assignment.

## Required Frameworks

Use both:

- Business Model Evolution Framework
- Digital Business Model Canvas

Use these frameworks to compare Deere before and after the platform shift. The Business Model Evolution Framework should help you explain the movement from product-centered value toward platform or ecosystem logic. The Digital Business Model Canvas should help you identify two or three business model elements that change, such as value proposition, customer relationship, channels, revenue, key resources, key activities, or partners.

## Prompt

With your assigned partner/team, write a concise response that addresses the following:

1. Explain Deere's traditional pre-platform business model.
2. Explain how precision agriculture changes Deere's business model.
3. Apply the Business Model Evolution Framework.
4. Use the Digital Business Model Canvas selectively to identify two or three business model elements that change.
5. Analyze the shift from the farmer/end-user perspective.
6. Analyze the shift from the Deere product-manager perspective.
7. Identify one disintermediation risk from startups or adjacent ag firms.
8. Identify one public Deere digital touchpoint or platform signal.
9. Recommend one business model move Deere should prioritize.
10. Propose one matched metric and explain why it fits.
11. Cite at least one source or assigned course example. Include source/date for current public claims.
12. Include AI/source transparency.

## Role Lens

Use two perspectives:

- Farmer/end user
- Deere product manager

From the farmer/end-user perspective, ask what job the farmer is trying to get done, what new value the platform creates beyond the equipment, and what new dependency, friction, switching cost, or trust concern the platform creates.

From the Deere product-manager perspective, ask what Deere should own, open, partner around, or protect. Think about how the same platform can create value for the farmer while changing Deere's revenue model, data position, partner ecosystem, and competitive exposure.

## Digital Touchpoint Cue

You do not need to log in, download an app, create an account, contact a dealer, or use Deere's private tools.

You may reference one public platform signal or digital touchpoint, such as Operations Center, precision-agriculture technology pages, connected equipment, app/mobile farm-management descriptions, API/developer ecosystem, service/support information, product ecosystem pages, or data-enabled workflow descriptions.

## Evidence Labels

Use these labels to keep your claims clear:

- Observed: sourced company facts, assigned course facts, or public Deere/platform evidence
- Inferred: reasonable business model or platform implications
- Recommended: your team's proposed business model move

Do not invent current Deere data, software revenue, partnerships, customer metrics, APIs, product capabilities, farmer adoption levels, or internal strategy.

## How the Work Will Be Evaluated

Your work will be evaluated using the four course discussion criteria:

1. Strategic Diagnosis and Critical Reasoning
2. Application of Course Frameworks with Specific Examples
3. Use of Examples and Evidence
4. Writing Clarity, Organization, and Proper Citation of Course Concepts

Your business model comparison, role perspectives, source evidence, recommendation, and metric provide evidence within those four criteria. They are not separate rubric categories.

## AI and Source Transparency

If your team uses AI, disclose the tool used, the purpose of use, your prompt or a concise prompt summary when required, and what your team checked, corrected, rejected, or rewrote.

AI output may help brainstorm or organize business model logic, but it is not evidence that Deere currently has a specific feature, partnership, metric, software revenue model, farmer adoption level, or internal strategy. Your team remains responsible for verifying current claims, citing sources, and writing the majority of the final submission.
