# Module 2 Discussion: Strategy Reimagined — Adobe Creative Cloud and Kinetic Strategy

## Purpose

This discussion asks you to evaluate Adobe's Creative Cloud pivot as an example of digital strategy moving from static planning to adaptability and velocity. Adobe's shift from boxed software to a subscription-based cloud model helps show why traditional strategy tools need to be updated for digital competition.

## Required Framework

- Kinetic Strategy Framework

Use the two core dimensions of the framework:

- Velocity: how quickly Adobe could sense, decide, ship, update, and learn.
- Adaptability: how effectively Adobe could change its business model, customer relationship, revenue model, and operating assumptions.

## Optional Supporting Frameworks

You may use one of these if it strengthens your analysis:

- Digital Horizon Planning
- Digital Business Model Canvas
- Reinvention Strategy Maturity Matrix
- Week 1 leadership concepts, if useful

Do not use classic SWOT or Porter's Five Forces as the main answer unless you explain how those tools need to be adapted for digital strategy.

## Prompt

With your assigned partner/team, write a concise response that addresses the following:

1. Explain the strategic problem Adobe faced with boxed software. What made the old model less effective in a digital environment?
2. Apply the Kinetic Strategy Framework to Adobe's Creative Cloud pivot.
3. Explain how the move changed Adobe's velocity and adaptability. Consider release cycles, learning, updates, customer relationships, revenue model, or operating assumptions.
4. Identify one traditional strategic assumption that Adobe's pivot challenged, such as long product cycles, stable industry boundaries, durable barriers to entry, or static competitive positioning.
5. Identify one customer-facing or operating touchpoint where the strategy likely showed up.
6. Use at least one cited source or assigned reading example. If using current public data, include source and date.
7. Include AI/source transparency.

## Role Lens

Use the Digital Strategy / General Manager lens. Think like the leader responsible for deciding whether Adobe's strategy created a more adaptive, measurable, and sustainable operating model.

## Digital Touchpoint Cue

You do not need to inspect a live Adobe website or app. You may reference one relevant touchpoint or operating implication, such as subscription pricing, Creative Cloud plans, cloud storage, cross-device access, customer accounts, continuous updates, usage signals, product adoption, or customer retention.

## Evidence Labels

Use evidence carefully:

- Observed: sourced company facts, assigned course facts, or public data.
- Inferred: reasonable strategic or operating implications based on evidence.
- Judged / Recommended: your team's evaluation of Adobe's strategic position.

Do not invent current data. AI output is not evidence of current Adobe performance, product capability, or metrics.
