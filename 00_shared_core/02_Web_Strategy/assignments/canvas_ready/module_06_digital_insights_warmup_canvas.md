# Module 6 Insight Warm-Up: Stitch Fix, Spotify, and the Insight Value Chain

## Purpose

This optional warm-up helps you practice Chapter 6's core idea: data creates strategic value only when it becomes insight, changes a decision, and produces a measurable outcome.

Use this as a midterm-prep activity unless your instructor gives different Canvas instructions.

## Required Framework

- Insight Value Chain

Use the Insight Value Chain to explain how a customer behavior signal can become an insight, how that insight can improve a decision, and how the outcome could be measured.

## Prompt

Compare Stitch Fix and Spotify by answering:

1. What is one customer behavior signal each company could observe?
2. How could that signal become an insight?
3. What business decision could that insight improve?
4. Where would the insight show up in the customer experience?
5. What metric would show whether the insight was useful?

Do not claim to know either company's internal algorithms, data systems, performance metrics, or private practices.

## Role Lens

Use a light role lens:

- Analytics & Experimentation
- Digital Product & Experience

Think about how a team would translate customer behavior into a product, personalization, lifecycle, or retention decision.

## Digital Touchpoint Cue

You may reference touchpoints such as:

- recommendation feed
- onboarding quiz
- taste or style profile
- playlist or product recommendation
- home screen
- email/push/message personalization
- retention or re-engagement message

Do not log in, download apps, enter personal information, or inspect private account experiences.

## Evidence Labels

- Observed: stated in course materials or visible in a public experience.
- Inferred: your reasonable conclusion about how data may support a decision.
- Recommended: your proposed decision, action, or metric.

## Suggested Format

1. Stitch Fix signal -> insight -> decision -> touchpoint -> metric
2. Spotify signal -> insight -> decision -> touchpoint -> metric
3. One sentence comparing the two
4. Sources and AI disclosure, if used

## AI / Source Transparency

AI may help you explain the difference between data, signal, insight, and decision. AI output is not evidence of current company algorithms, systems, metrics, or performance.

If you use AI, disclose the tool, the purpose of use, and what you checked, corrected, rejected, or rewrote. If you use outside sources, cite them and include the source date for current claims.
