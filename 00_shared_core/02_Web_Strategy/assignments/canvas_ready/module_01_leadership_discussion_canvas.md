# Module 1 Discussion: Digital Leadership and Organizational Readiness

## Purpose

This discussion compares two digital leadership transformations: Microsoft under Satya Nadella after 2014 and Nike's Consumer Direct Acceleration program after 2018. Your goal is to connect leadership choices to organizational readiness, digital operating models, and customer or business outcomes.

Keep the response focused on leadership and readiness rather than a full company history.

## Required Frameworks

Use both required lenses:

- Overclocked Chapter 1 digital leadership/change framework
- Gartner: Seven Traits of Highly Successful Digital Leaders

Use at least two Gartner traits and at least two Overclocked Chapter 1 leadership/change concepts. Apply the frameworks to the companies; do not just define the terms.

## Prompt

With your assigned partner/team, write a concise response that addresses the following:

1. Compare Microsoft/Nadella and Nike CDA. How did Nadella's leadership at Microsoft and Nike's Consumer Direct Acceleration each try to shift the organization toward a more digital operating model?
2. Apply the required frameworks. Use at least two Gartner traits and at least two Overclocked Chapter 1 leadership/change concepts to compare the two companies.
3. Evaluate organizational readiness. Which company showed stronger alignment between leadership mindset and organizational readiness? Explain your reasoning.
4. Use evidence. Include one cited public data point for Microsoft and one cited public data point for Nike. Provide the source and date for each data point.
5. Connect leadership to digital operations. Identify one way the leadership shift likely showed up in a digital operating model or customer-facing system, such as cloud services, an app ecosystem, direct-to-consumer channels, owned media, personalization, data use, product roadmap, or CRM/customer relationship management.

## Role Lens

Use the Digital Strategy / General Manager lens. Think like leaders responsible for aligning vision, culture, teams, systems, and customer outcomes.

## Evidence Labels

Use evidence carefully:

- Observed: sourced company facts, data points, or assigned course facts.
- Inferred: reasonable leadership or operating implications based on evidence.
- Judged / Recommended: your team's evaluation of which company showed stronger alignment.

Do not invent current data. AI output is not evidence of current company performance or current company capabilities.
