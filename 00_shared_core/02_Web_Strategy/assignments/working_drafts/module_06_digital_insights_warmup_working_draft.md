## Module Objective Alignment

Syllabus objectives:

- Understand and leverage core insights frameworks for use in strategy.
- Apply the insights to strategic outcomes.

Chapter 6 mastery objectives supported by this scaffold:

- Convert enterprise-wide data into actionable insights using the Insight Value Chain.
- Design cross-functional activation systems that connect insight to measurable outcomes.
- Evaluate the data ecosystem across qualitative and quantitative inputs.
- Apply insight-generation models to real-world business challenges.

This warm-up supports the module by helping students practice the difference between collecting data, interpreting a signal, forming an insight, changing a decision, and measuring whether that decision improved a customer or business outcome. It also reinforces the midterm expectation that evidence should support a strategic argument rather than become a list of facts.

## Role and Touchpoint Cue

Use a light role lens:

- Analytics & Experimentation
- Digital Product & Experience
- CRM, Lifecycle & Loyalty as a supporting lens

Students should think like a team trying to turn customer behavior into a better decision. The role lens should help them ask what evidence matters, what product or lifecycle decision could change, and what metric would show whether the insight was useful.

Relevant touchpoints:

- recommendation feed
- onboarding quiz
- style or taste profile
- playlist/recommendation experience
- home screen
- email/push/message personalization

Do not require login, account creation, app download, purchase, personal data entry, or private account inspection.

## Student-Facing Prompt Draft

This optional warm-up helps you practice Chapter 6's main idea: data creates strategic value only when it becomes insight, changes a decision, and produces a measurable outcome.

Compare Stitch Fix and Spotify as examples of insight-driven digital experiences. For each company:

1. Identify one customer behavior signal the company could observe.
2. Explain how that signal could become an insight.
3. Identify one business decision the insight could improve.
4. Identify one customer-facing touchpoint where the insight would show up.
5. Recommend one metric that would show whether the insight was useful.
6. Apply the Insight Value Chain to connect the signal, insight, decision, action, and outcome.
7. Include source and AI transparency if AI or external sources are used.

This is a learning scaffold / midterm-prep activity, not a new graded discussion unless your instructor says otherwise.

## Evidence Discipline

Require students to distinguish:

- Observed: stated in course materials or visible in a public experience.
- Inferred: a reasonable conclusion about how data may support decisions.
- Recommended: the team's proposed decision, action, or metric.

Students must not claim to know internal algorithms, internal data pipelines, proprietary models, private performance metrics, or current company systems. They may discuss plausible insight logic, but they should label it as inference.

AI output may help students explain the difference between data, signal, insight, and decision. AI output is not evidence of current Stitch Fix or Spotify capabilities, algorithms, systems, metrics, or performance.

## Strong-Answer Indicators

- Distinguishes data from insight.
- Shows how insight changes a decision.
- Connects data to a customer-facing touchpoint.
- Proposes a matched metric.
- Avoids proprietary claims.
- Applies the Insight Value Chain rather than merely naming it.
- Uses Stitch Fix as the primary Chapter 6 anchor and Spotify as a familiar comparison when appropriate.
- Labels observed facts, reasonable inferences, and recommendations.

## Weak-Answer Indicators

- Says "more data is better."
- Confuses dashboard reporting with insight.
- Describes personalization without identifying a decision.
- Assumes internal algorithms, private data access, or proprietary systems.
- Proposes vague metrics.
- Treats AI output as evidence.
- Makes unsupported current claims about Stitch Fix or Spotify.
- Turns the warm-up into a technical AI/ML explanation rather than a strategy exercise.
