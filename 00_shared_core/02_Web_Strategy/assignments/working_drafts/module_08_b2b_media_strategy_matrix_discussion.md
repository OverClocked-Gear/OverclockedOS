# Module 8 Discussion: B2B Buyer Behavior, Platforms, and the Media Strategy Matrix

## Draft Status

This is an instructor-facing working draft for the existing Module 8 discussion. It revises the B2B platform/marketplace assignment into a parallel B2B application of the Media Strategy Matrix while preserving Module 8's buyer-journey and platform/ecosystem focus. It is not a new assignment, not a quiz, and not a Brand Pod.

## Course Placement

- Course: Web Strategy
- Module: Module 8 - B2B: Platforms and Marketplaces
- Audience: undergraduate
- Delivery: asynchronous
- Assessment type: existing team discussion
- Team structure: assigned partner/team of two, per syllabus

## Module Objective Alignment

Syllabus objectives:

- Define the Digital-Driven B2B Path to Purchase.
- Define unique challenges of incumbents vs. start-ups.
- Evaluate how digital platforms transform traditional buyer-seller relationships in B2B contexts.
- Apply ecosystem thinking to complex B2B value chains.

This revision supports those objectives by asking students to apply the same Media Strategy Matrix used in Module 7 to a B2B buyer journey. Students must account for buying committees, trust/proof requirements, content role, lead nurture, platform value, and metrics.

## Framework Contract

Required:

- Media Strategy Matrix

Students must use the Media Strategy Matrix to classify a B2B buyer state using two dimensions:

- Brand awareness: low or high
- Purchase/evaluation intent: low or high

They should use the quadrant to explain buying committee needs, the content role, proof requirements, the lead-nurture path, and the recommended improvement.

Optional supporting frameworks:

- B2B Platform Evolution Framework, if used to explain Salesforce as a platform/ecosystem.
- B2B Platform Maturity Matrix, if used briefly to evaluate platform depth.
- Industry Operating System Framework, if used to explain ecosystem coordination.
- Insight Value Chain, if used briefly to explain what buyer signal should inform nurture or prioritization.

Do not force:

- full B2B platform redesign
- private procurement-system analysis
- form submission or demo-request completion
- full infrastructure stack analysis
- consumer marketplace analogies as the main answer
- AI prompt-iteration assignment

## Brand and Surface Guidance

Recommended B2B brand: Salesforce.

Salesforce is appropriate because it gives students accessible public examples of B2B product positioning, industry solutions, customer proof, learning content, events/webinars, partner/platform language, and lead-nurture surfaces without requiring access to private systems.

Possible public surfaces:

- product pages
- industry solution pages
- customer stories
- research reports
- Trailhead or learning content
- demo-request pages, without submitting a form
- events or webinar pages
- pricing or contact-sales pages, without contacting sales

Do not require form submission, demo request, account creation, sales contact, login, purchase, or private platform access.

## Role and Touchpoint Cue

Use a light role lens:

- Performance Marketing
- CRM, Lifecycle & Loyalty
- Digital Strategy / General Manager
- Platform, Technology & Operations as a dependency only

Students should think like a B2B growth and strategy team deciding how to help a buying committee move from awareness to evaluation, proof, consensus, and qualified next step. The analysis should recognize that B2B buyers often need education, credibility, risk reduction, stakeholder alignment, and platform/ecosystem proof.

## Student-Facing Prompt Draft

Use the Media Strategy Matrix to analyze a Salesforce B2B buyer journey.

Choose one buyer state:

- Low awareness / low evaluation intent
- Low awareness / high evaluation intent
- High awareness / low evaluation intent
- High awareness / high evaluation intent

Then answer:

1. Identify the buyer state and explain the B2B behavior in that quadrant.
2. Identify the likely buying committee needs.
3. Identify one public Salesforce surface that fits the buyer state.
4. Explain the content role for that surface.
5. Identify the trust or proof requirement.
6. Explain the likely lead-nurture path or next step, without submitting a form.
7. Recommend one improvement to the touchpoint, proof, or nurture path.
8. Propose one matched metric and explain why it fits.
9. Use evidence labels: Observed, Inferred, and Recommended.
10. Cite any current public claim with source and date.
11. Include AI/source transparency if AI or outside sources are used.

## Evidence Discipline

Students must label:

- Observed: what is stated in course materials or visible on a public surface.
- Inferred: a reasonable conclusion about buying committee needs, proof requirements, lead-nurture logic, or platform/ecosystem implications.
- Recommended: the team's proposed improvement and metric.

Students must not invent current Salesforce pages, features, offers, campaigns, targeting, personalization, analytics, customer results, metrics, platform capabilities, or performance. AI output is not evidence of current brand capabilities or strategy.

## Suggested Response Structure

1. Buyer state / Media Strategy Matrix quadrant
2. Buying committee needs
3. Public surface and content role
4. Trust/proof requirement
5. Lead-nurture path or next step
6. Recommended improvement
7. Metric and why it fits
8. Sources and AI disclosure

## Strong-Answer Indicators

- Uses one Media Strategy Matrix quadrant clearly.
- Explains how B2B awareness and evaluation intent differ from consumer intent.
- Identifies buying committee needs and risk concerns.
- Connects the quadrant to a relevant public Salesforce surface.
- Explains the content role and trust/proof requirement.
- Describes a plausible lead-nurture path without requiring form submission.
- Recommends one focused improvement.
- Selects a metric that matches the recommendation.
- Distinguishes observed evidence from inference.

## Weak-Answer Indicators

- Treats Salesforce as a simple consumer purchase.
- Ignores buying committees, proof, risk, or stakeholder alignment.
- Lists Salesforce products without explaining buyer journey logic.
- Assumes private lead-scoring, targeting, conversion, or CRM data.
- Requires a demo request or sales contact.
- Uses consumer marketplace logic as the main analysis.
- Proposes a vague metric without explaining why it fits.
- Uses AI output as evidence.

## Workload Estimate

Target 45-60 minutes total for a two-person asynchronous discussion, including selecting one quadrant, reviewing a public/course-described surface, drafting, checking evidence labels, and revising. This should replace the normal Module 8 discussion work, not add another activity.

## AI and Source Transparency

If students use AI, they should disclose the tool used, the purpose of use, and what they checked, corrected, rejected, or rewrote. AI may help brainstorm B2B buying committee logic or clarify differences between awareness and evaluation intent. AI output is not evidence of current Salesforce capabilities, campaign strategy, targeting, personalization, analytics, lead scoring, platform performance, or customer outcomes.

## Module 7 / Module 8 Sequence Note

Modules 7 and 8 use the same Media Strategy Matrix so students can compare B2C and B2B digital behavior with a shared structure. Module 7 emphasizes individual consumer motivation, subscription or usage intent, lifecycle friction, and retention metrics. Module 8 emphasizes buying committees, trust/proof, lead nurture, platform/ecosystem value, and qualified pipeline metrics. The shared framework creates consistency, while the audience logic, touchpoints, evidence burden, metrics, and strategic risks differ.
