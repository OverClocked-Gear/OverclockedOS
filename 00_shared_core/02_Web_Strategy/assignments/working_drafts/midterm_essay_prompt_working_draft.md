# Midterm Essay: Connected Fitness at a Strategic Crossroads

## Draft Status

This is an instructor-facing working draft for the 100-point, 60-minute Web Strategy midterm essay. It revises the existing connected-fitness scenario with clearer framework boundaries, response structure, evidence expectations, timing guidance, and rubric alignment.

The revision preserves the original "Connecting the Dots" purpose while shifting the assessment from breadth to synthesis. Students should use a small number of frameworks well rather than mention every concept from the first half of the course.

## Course Placement

- Course: Web Strategy
- Assessment: Quiz/Midterm
- Timing: Module 6 / Week 7
- Format: one timed essay question in ICON
- Time limit: 60 minutes
- Point value: 100 points
- Coverage: Chapter 1-5 synthesis as the required framework universe
- Chapter 6 / Insight Value Chain: optional instructor bridge only, because the midterm sits in Module 6

## Assessment Purpose

The midterm tests whether students can:

- connect course concepts across Chapters 1-5
- frame the central strategic problem or opportunity
- make a well-reasoned strategic argument
- apply frameworks rather than list them
- use scenario evidence and course concepts
- recognize tradeoffs
- close with a clear recommendation and conclusion

## Scenario

A fictional connected-fitness company, PulseFit, built its early growth around premium home exercise equipment, a subscription content app, and a community layer that let members join challenges, follow instructors, and share progress. Rapid adoption helped the company grow quickly, but the market has changed.

Growth is slowing as premium hardware becomes easier to copy and customers question whether expensive equipment is necessary. Churn is rising among subscribers who no longer use the app consistently. Lower-cost app-only fitness competitors are attracting customers who want flexibility without buying hardware. Wearable ecosystems are becoming more central to health and activity tracking. Creator-led fitness alternatives are building loyal audiences outside traditional fitness platforms. At the same time, employer wellness programs, insurance partnerships, and health-related data partnerships could create new growth paths if PulseFit can execute responsibly.

Leadership is divided. One group wants to defend the hardware-centered model by launching new equipment and expanding into new markets. Another group wants to reinvent PulseFit as a broader digital fitness and wellness platform built around the app, community, data-enabled personalization, partnerships, and ongoing engagement. A third group argues for a hybrid path: protect the premium hardware business while building a more flexible platform around content, community, wearables, and partnerships.

PulseFit faces finite resources, inconsistent customer insight, infrastructure limitations, slowing engagement, and weak cross-functional alignment. The executive team needs a strategic recommendation about whether to defend the hardware-centered model, reinvent as a digital fitness/wellness platform, or pursue a hybrid path.

## Framework Contract

### Required Synthesis Framework

- Kinetic Strategy Framework

Students must use Kinetic Strategy to evaluate whether the company's challenge is primarily:

- low adaptability
- low velocity
- both
- or a mismatch between the two

### Required Supporting Framework Choice

Students must choose two of the following Chapter 1-5 framework areas:

1. Digital Leadership / Change Readiness
   Use this if leadership mindset, culture, organizational readiness, or change capacity is central to the diagnosis.

2. Five Catalysts of Disruption / Diffusion-Disruption logic
   Use this if adoption, market timing, customer behavior, or competitive disruption is central to the diagnosis.

3. TOE Framework or Integrated Tech Stack
   Use this if infrastructure, internal systems, data, digital experience, or technology readiness is central to the diagnosis.

4. Business Model Evolution Framework or Digital Business Model Canvas
   Use this if revenue model, customer relationship, platform strategy, partners, monetization, or value creation is central to the diagnosis.

### Optional

- Insight Value Chain, only if the instructor chooses to include Chapter 6 as a light bridge or if students use it briefly to explain what data/insight capability is required for the recommendation.

### Do Not Force

- every Chapter 1-5 framework
- full infrastructure stack analysis
- full business model canvas
- full adoption curve
- current company research
- live digital surface inspection
- LLM prompting

Strong work applies a small number of frameworks deeply. Weak work name-drops many frameworks without using them to make a diagnosis or recommendation.

## Role Lens

Use:

- Digital Strategy / General Manager

Students should write as if advising the executive team. Their job is to diagnose the problem, select the most useful frameworks, prioritize tradeoffs, and recommend a path forward.

## Touchpoint / Operating Context Cue

Students may reference the connected-fitness ecosystem as an operating context:

- equipment
- app
- subscription content
- community features
- wearable integration
- lifecycle engagement
- retention messaging
- data/insight loop
- partnership ecosystem

Do not require live website/app inspection.

## Evidence Expectations

The exam is based on the scenario and course concepts unless the instructor explicitly permits outside sources. Students should use scenario facts and course concepts as evidence, cite or name course frameworks/concepts clearly, and avoid inventing current market data.

Students should distinguish:

- Observed: facts given in the scenario or assigned course material
- Inferred: reasonable conclusions from those facts
- Recommended: the student's proposed action

The student's argument should distinguish facts in the scenario from reasonable inference and recommendation.

## Student-Facing Prompt Draft

PulseFit is a fictional connected-fitness company at a strategic crossroads. It built early growth around premium home equipment, a subscription content app, and a community/social motivation layer. Growth is now slowing. Hardware is becoming easier to copy, churn is rising, lower-cost app-only competitors are attracting customers, wearable ecosystems are becoming more central to health tracking, creator-led fitness alternatives are gaining loyalty, and employer/wellness/insurance partnerships may create new opportunities.

PulseFit's leadership must decide whether to defend the hardware-centered model, reinvent as a broader digital fitness and wellness platform, or pursue a hybrid path.

In your timed essay:

1. Diagnose the company's central strategic problem or opportunity.
2. Apply the Kinetic Strategy Framework to evaluate adaptability and velocity.
3. Choose two supporting Chapter 1-5 frameworks and use them to explain root causes, tradeoffs, or strategic options.
4. Recommend one primary path forward.
5. Identify two or three implementation priorities.
6. Identify one metric or success signal.
7. Close with a clear conclusion.

## Required Response Structure

Students may follow this structure:

1. Problem/opportunity diagnosis
2. Kinetic Strategy analysis
3. Supporting framework analysis
4. Strategic recommendation
5. Implementation priorities
6. Metric / success signal
7. Conclusion

## Timing Guidance

Suggested 60-minute allocation:

- 5 minutes: read and outline
- 10 minutes: diagnose the problem/opportunity
- 15 minutes: apply Kinetic Strategy
- 15 minutes: apply two supporting frameworks
- 10 minutes: recommendation, metric, and conclusion
- 5 minutes: revise for clarity

## Canonical Rubric Alignment

Use only the four canonical criteria:

1. Strategic Diagnosis and Critical Reasoning
2. Application of Course Frameworks with Specific Examples
3. Use of Examples and Evidence
4. Writing Clarity, Organization, and Proper Citation of Course Concepts

| Prompt requirement | Applicable rubric criterion | Evidence expected | Common grading concern |
|---|---|---|---|
| Diagnose the central problem or opportunity | Strategic Diagnosis and Critical Reasoning | Clear prioritization of the main strategic issue rather than a list of symptoms | Student summarizes the scenario without identifying root cause or central tension |
| Apply Kinetic Strategy | Application of Course Frameworks with Specific Examples; Strategic Diagnosis and Critical Reasoning | Adaptability and velocity used to explain PulseFit's strategic position | Student says "be faster" without distinguishing adaptability from velocity |
| Choose two supporting frameworks | Application of Course Frameworks with Specific Examples | Two well-fit frameworks applied to root causes, tradeoffs, or options | Student mentions many frameworks but applies none deeply |
| Use scenario evidence | Use of Examples and Evidence | Scenario facts used as evidence for diagnosis and recommendation | Student invents current market data or unsupported facts |
| Recommend one path forward | Strategic Diagnosis and Critical Reasoning | Clear choice among defend, reinvent, or hybrid, with rationale | Student gives vague "do more digital" advice |
| Identify implementation priorities | Strategic Diagnosis and Critical Reasoning; Use of Examples and Evidence | Two or three concrete priorities tied to the recommendation | Student lists unrelated actions without sequencing or tradeoffs |
| Identify one metric or success signal | Use of Examples and Evidence | Metric tied to the recommendation and success logic | Student omits a metric or uses a vague metric without explanation |
| Write a clear conclusion | Writing Clarity, Organization, and Proper Citation of Course Concepts | Concise closing that reinforces the argument and recommendation | Essay ends abruptly or repeats the scenario |
| Name course frameworks/concepts clearly | Writing Clarity, Organization, and Proper Citation of Course Concepts | Accurate use of framework names and course concept language | Student uses unclear or inaccurate framework names |

Do not create new rubric categories.

## Strong-Answer Indicators

- identifies one central strategic problem or opportunity
- avoids simply summarizing the scenario
- uses Kinetic Strategy as the organizing diagnosis
- distinguishes adaptability from velocity
- selects supporting frameworks that fit the issue
- explains root causes and tradeoffs
- connects leadership, disruption, infrastructure, and business model logic where relevant
- recommends one clear strategic path
- includes two or three implementation priorities
- provides one relevant metric or success signal
- closes with a clear conclusion

## Weak-Answer Indicators

- lists many frameworks without applying them
- treats every chapter concept as equally important
- gives a generic "do more digital" recommendation
- confuses symptoms with root causes
- ignores customer behavior or business model pressure
- ignores organizational readiness or infrastructure constraints
- makes unsupported claims not in the scenario
- provides no metric
- lacks a clear conclusion
