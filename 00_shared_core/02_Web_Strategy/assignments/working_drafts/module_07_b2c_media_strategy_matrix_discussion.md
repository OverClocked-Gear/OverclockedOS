# Module 7 Discussion: B2C Digital Consumer Behavior and the Media Strategy Matrix

## Draft Status

This is an instructor-facing working draft for the existing Module 7 discussion. It revises the source Media Strategy Matrix exercise into a focused B2C consumer-behavior assignment. It is not a new assignment, not a quiz, and not a Brand Pod.

## Course Placement

- Course: Web Strategy
- Module: Module 7 - B2C: The Digital Consumer
- Audience: undergraduate
- Delivery: asynchronous
- Assessment type: existing team discussion
- Team structure: assigned partner/team of two, per syllabus

## Module Objective Alignment

Syllabus objectives:

- Understand the foundational drivers of consumer digital behaviors.
- Gain the ability to contrast digital with traditional behaviors related to marketing strategies and tactics.

This revision supports those objectives by asking students to connect consumer awareness, purchase/subscription intent, touchpoint needs, friction, and metrics. Students apply one shared framework while focusing specifically on the B2C journey.

## Framework Contract

Required:

- Media Strategy Matrix

Students must use the Media Strategy Matrix to classify a B2C audience state using two dimensions:

- Brand awareness: low or high
- Purchase/subscription intent: low or high

They should use the quadrant to explain the audience's likely behavior, what content or message is needed, which touchpoint matters, and what improvement should be prioritized.

Optional supporting frameworks:

- Modern Consumer Journey Loop, if used to explain nonlinear consumer behavior.
- Digital Consumer Archetypes, if used to describe the consumer's evidence needs or motivation.
- Insight Value Chain, if used briefly to explain what signal would help the team learn.

Do not force:

- Module 3 disruption/adoption analysis
- full Tesla competitive analysis
- full paid/owned/earned media plan
- AI prompt-iteration assignment
- private account or app inspection

## Brand and Surface Guidance

Recommended B2C brand: Spotify.

Spotify is stronger than Tesla for Module 7 because it naturally foregrounds consumer behavior, personalization, discovery, subscription choice, playlist/recommendation experience, and retention/lifecycle logic. It also avoids repeating the earlier Module 3 Tesla disruption/adoption assignment.

Tesla can remain an instructor alternative if the goal is a high-consideration consumer journey. If Tesla is used, frame the analysis around consumer research, direct-to-consumer product comparison/configuration, ownership ecosystem expectations, and app/connected-product expectations. Do not repeat the Module 3 disruption/adoption analysis.

Possible public surfaces for Spotify:

- public plan or subscription information
- public product or feature descriptions
- public playlist, discovery, or personalization descriptions
- public help or support content
- public brand/content pages

Do not require login, app download, private account access, payment, or personal data entry.

## Role and Touchpoint Cue

Use a light role lens:

- Digital Product & Experience
- CRM, Lifecycle & Loyalty
- Performance Marketing as support

Students should think like the team deciding how to move a consumer from attention to consideration, trial, subscription, repeat use, or retention. The touchpoint should be public or course-described, and the analysis should focus on what the consumer needs at that audience state.

## Student-Facing Prompt Draft

Use the Media Strategy Matrix to analyze a Spotify consumer journey.

Choose one audience state:

- Low awareness / low intent
- Low awareness / high intent
- High awareness / low intent
- High awareness / high intent

Then answer:

1. Identify the audience state and explain the consumer behavior in that quadrant.
2. Identify one public or course-described touchpoint where this audience might encounter Spotify.
3. Explain the touchpoint's role in the journey.
4. Identify the message or content need for that audience state.
5. Identify one likely friction point.
6. Recommend one improvement to the touchpoint, message, or lifecycle path.
7. Propose one matched metric and explain why it fits.
8. Use evidence labels: Observed, Inferred, and Recommended.
9. Cite any current public claim with source and date.
10. Include AI/source transparency if AI or outside sources are used.

## Evidence Discipline

Students must label:

- Observed: what is stated in course materials or visible on a public surface.
- Inferred: a reasonable conclusion about consumer behavior, motivation, friction, or lifecycle need.
- Recommended: the team's proposed improvement and metric.

Students must not invent current Spotify pages, features, offers, campaigns, targeting, personalization practices, analytics, metrics, or performance. AI output is not evidence of current brand capabilities or strategy.

## Suggested Response Structure

1. Audience state / Media Strategy Matrix quadrant
2. Consumer behavior diagnosis
3. Touchpoint role
4. Message or content need
5. Likely friction
6. Recommended improvement
7. Metric and why it fits
8. Sources and AI disclosure

## Strong-Answer Indicators

- Uses one Media Strategy Matrix quadrant clearly.
- Explains how awareness and intent shape the consumer's needs.
- Connects the quadrant to a realistic public touchpoint.
- Identifies a specific message/content need and friction.
- Recommends one focused improvement.
- Selects a metric that matches the recommendation.
- Distinguishes observed evidence from inference.
- Avoids unsupported current claims about Spotify.

## Weak-Answer Indicators

- Summarizes Spotify without analyzing consumer behavior.
- Uses the same recommendation for every quadrant.
- Treats personalization as a vague good rather than a decision.
- Repeats Module 3 Tesla disruption logic if Tesla is used.
- Claims knowledge of current targeting, algorithms, campaigns, or performance without evidence.
- Proposes a vague metric such as "engagement" without defining the behavior.
- Uses AI output as evidence.

## Workload Estimate

Target 45-60 minutes total for a two-person asynchronous discussion, including selecting one quadrant, reviewing a public/course-described touchpoint, drafting, checking evidence labels, and revising. This should replace the normal Module 7 discussion work, not add another activity.

## AI and Source Transparency

If students use AI, they should disclose the tool used, the purpose of use, and what they checked, corrected, rejected, or rewrote. AI may help brainstorm audience-state logic or clarify the difference between awareness and intent. AI output is not evidence of current Spotify capabilities, campaign strategy, targeting, personalization, analytics, or performance.

## Module 7 / Module 8 Sequence Note

Modules 7 and 8 use the same Media Strategy Matrix so students can compare B2C and B2B digital behavior with a shared structure. Module 7 emphasizes individual consumer motivation, subscription or usage intent, lifecycle friction, and retention metrics. Module 8 emphasizes buying committees, trust/proof, lead nurture, platform/ecosystem value, and qualified pipeline metrics. The shared framework creates consistency, while the audience logic, touchpoints, evidence burden, metrics, and strategic risks differ.
