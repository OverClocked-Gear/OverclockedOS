# Module 5 Discussion: Deere — From Equipment Manufacturer to Precision Agriculture Platform

## Draft Status

This is an instructor-facing working draft and a moderate revision of the existing Module 5 discussion. It replaces the broader Peloton/Deere comparison with a Deere-only pre-platform/post-platform business model analysis. It is not a Brand Pod and not a new assignment.

The revised discussion preserves the original business model focus while reducing workload, strengthening framework transparency, and giving students a clearer platform strategy scenario.

## Course Placement

- Course: Web Strategy
- Module: Module 5 — Digital Business Models
- Audience: undergraduate
- Delivery: asynchronous
- Assessment type: existing team discussion
- Team structure: assigned partner/team of two, per syllabus

## Original Assignment Intent

The original prompt asked students to:

- compare hardware-to-data/platform business models
- use business model frameworks
- connect to prior weeks' strategy, disruption, and infrastructure concepts
- support with evidence

The revision narrows to Deere because it:

- reduces workload for a weekly asynchronous discussion
- creates a clearer business model contrast between equipment-centered and platform-enabled logic
- makes platform evolution easier to analyze through one company
- avoids a superficial comparison between unrelated companies

## Module Objective Alignment

Module 5 syllabus objectives:

- The role of business models in developing strategy
- Understand the range of business models across content, commerce, and community-based enterprises
- Identify business model opportunities within an existing organization and industry

The revised prompt supports these objectives by asking students to analyze how Deere's value creation and value capture may change as precision agriculture shifts the company from an equipment-centered model toward a platform-enabled model. Students compare business model logic, evaluate the farmer/end-user value proposition, and recommend one business model move Deere should prioritize.

## Scenario: Deere as a Platform Business

Deere has historically been understood as an equipment manufacturer supported by dealers, service, parts, financing, and machine performance. Precision agriculture changes the strategic question. Connected machines, Operations Center, data, software, APIs, and farm-management workflows can shift Deere from selling equipment to orchestrating a platform that supports farming decisions.

Students should evaluate:

- Deere before the platform shift
- Deere after the platform shift
- the value created for the farmer/end user
- the business model implications for Deere as product/platform manager
- the risk that startups or adjacent ag firms capture the farm decision layer if Deere does not continuously adapt

Clarifications:

- The assignment does not require students to become ag-tech experts.
- Students should not speculate about Deere's internal systems or private customer data.
- Students should cite any current public claims with source and date.
- Disintermediation should be treated as a strategic risk, not as a claim that Deere is currently being displaced.

## Framework Contract

### Required Framework

- Business Model Evolution Framework

### Required Supporting Tool

- Digital Business Model Canvas

Students should use the frameworks to compare Deere's pre-platform and post-platform business model.

### Optional Extensions

- Kinetic Strategy Framework, only if used briefly to evaluate whether Deere is adapting fast enough
- Digital Horizon Planning, only if used briefly to separate current model optimization from future platform options
- Strategic Capabilities Matrix, if useful for explaining platform defensibility or capability gaps

### Do Not Force

- full infrastructure stack analysis
- full ag-tech industry scan
- speculative startup scouting
- full business model redesign
- AI/blockchain future-state analysis unless directly tied to the business model
- Peloton comparison

### Framework Use Expectations

Students must use the frameworks to:

- compare old and new business model logic
- identify what changes in value proposition, customer relationship, channels, revenue, resources, activities, and partners
- make a business model judgment
- connect the business model shift to one operating dependency and one metric

## Role and Touchpoint Cue

Use a two-perspective lens within one team submission.

Perspective 1:

- Farmer / end user

Perspective 2:

- Deere product manager

Supporting role lenses:

- Digital Strategy / General Manager
- Digital Product & Experience
- Analytics & Experimentation
- Platform, Technology & Operations as a dependency only

From the farmer/end-user perspective, students should ask:

- What job is the farmer trying to get done?
- What new value does the platform create beyond the equipment?
- What new dependency, friction, switching cost, or trust concern does the platform create?
- What would make the farmer adopt and keep using the platform?

From the Deere product-manager perspective, students should ask:

- What must Deere own, open, partner around, or protect?
- What value is created by data, software, connected machines, partner integrations, or workflows?
- Where could Deere be disintermediated by startups or adjacent firms?
- How can Deere capture value without weakening farmer trust?

Students may reference one public Deere platform signal or digital touchpoint, such as:

- Operations Center
- precision-agriculture technology page
- connected equipment
- app/mobile farm management
- API/developer ecosystem
- digital service/support experience
- product ecosystem page
- dealer/service integration
- data-enabled farm-management workflow

Do not require login, app download, account creation, dealer contact, or private platform access.

## Student-Facing Prompt Draft

For this discussion, analyze Deere as a company moving from an equipment-centered business model toward a precision-agriculture platform model. Your goal is to explain how the business model changes when Deere's value is not only the machine, but also the connected data, software, workflows, support, and ecosystem around the machine.

With your assigned partner/team, write a concise response that addresses the following:

1. Explain Deere's pre-platform business model.
2. Explain Deere's post-platform or platform-enabled business model.
3. Apply the Business Model Evolution Framework.
4. Use the Digital Business Model Canvas selectively to identify what changes across value proposition, customer relationship, revenue, resources, activities, or partners.
5. Analyze the shift from the farmer/end-user perspective.
6. Analyze the shift from the Deere product-manager perspective.
7. Identify one risk of disintermediation from ag-tech startups or adjacent firms.
8. Identify one public Deere digital touchpoint or platform signal.
9. Recommend one business model move Deere should prioritize.
10. Propose one matched metric and explain why it fits.
11. Use at least one cited source or assigned course example. If using current public data, include source and date.
12. Include AI/source transparency.

## Evidence Discipline

Students should distinguish:

- Observed: sourced company facts, assigned course facts, or public Deere/platform evidence
- Inferred: reasonable business model or platform implications
- Recommended: the team's proposed business model move

Students should not invent current Deere data, software revenue, partnerships, customer metrics, APIs, product capabilities, or internal strategy.

AI output may help students understand platform logic or compare business model patterns, but it is not evidence of current Deere capabilities.

## Strong-Answer Indicators

- clearly distinguishes pre-platform and post-platform business models
- applies the Business Model Evolution Framework rather than merely naming it
- uses the Digital Business Model Canvas selectively
- explains how farmer value changes
- explains how Deere's product/platform management challenge changes
- identifies a realistic disintermediation risk
- connects the business model to one public digital touchpoint or platform signal
- recommends a specific business model move
- proposes a metric tied directly to the recommendation
- cites current claims with source/date
- discloses AI/source use transparently

## Weak-Answer Indicators

- summarizes Deere as a company without business model analysis
- treats the platform only as a website or app
- describes precision farming technology without explaining value capture
- ignores the farmer/end-user perspective
- ignores Deere's platform/product-manager perspective
- makes unsupported claims about Deere data, software revenue, APIs, partnerships, or farmer adoption
- treats disintermediation as certain rather than as a strategic risk
- proposes generic "use more data" recommendations
- selects a vague or unrelated metric
- uses AI output as evidence without verification

## Common Student Misconceptions

- a platform is just a website or app
- Deere's business model is only equipment sales
- more data automatically creates value
- farmer adoption is guaranteed if the technology is advanced
- recurring revenue is always good for customers
- ecosystem openness and platform control are not tradeoffs
- disintermediation only comes from direct competitors
- a metric must already be publicly available
