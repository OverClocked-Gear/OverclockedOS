## Original Assignment Intent

The original prompt asks students to compare two digital leadership and transformation efforts:

- Microsoft under Satya Nadella after 2014
- Nike's Consumer Direct Acceleration program after 2018

Students apply Gartner's Seven Traits of Highly Successful Digital Leaders and Overclocked Chapter 1 change/leadership concepts, evaluate the relationship between leadership mindset and organizational readiness, and support the comparison with public data.

## Module Objective Alignment

Module 1 syllabus objectives:

- Understand the dynamic definition of digital.
- Appreciate the roles of change, expertise & catalyst play in leadership.

The revised prompt supports these objectives by asking students to treat digital as an operating model rather than a technology label. Students compare how leadership interventions attempted to change culture, decision-making, systems, and customer-facing outcomes. The assignment also asks students to connect leadership behavior to digital operating models and customer/business outcomes through one light touchpoint or operating implication.

## Student-Facing Prompt Draft

Chapter 1 of Overclocked argues that digital transformation is not only a technology challenge. It is also a leadership, culture, and change-readiness challenge. Digital leaders must translate strategy into action, align teams across silos, use data to guide decisions, and build organizations that can keep adapting.

For this discussion, your team will compare two digital leadership efforts:

- Microsoft under Satya Nadella after 2014
- Nike's Consumer Direct Acceleration program after 2018

With your assigned partner, write a concise team response that addresses the following:

1. Compare the leadership interventions. How did Nadella's leadership at Microsoft and Nike's Consumer Direct Acceleration each try to shift the organization toward a more digital operating model?
2. Apply the required frameworks. Use at least two Gartner traits and at least two Overclocked Chapter 1 leadership/change concepts. Use the frameworks to explain how the two companies differed, not just to define the terms.
3. Evaluate organizational readiness. Which company showed stronger alignment between leadership mindset and organizational readiness? Explain your reasoning.
4. Support with evidence. Use one cited public data point for Microsoft and one cited public data point for Nike. Include the source and date for each data point.
5. Identify one leadership-to-digital-touchpoint or operating-model implication. Explain one way the leadership shift likely showed up in a digital operating model or customer-facing system, such as cloud services, an app ecosystem, direct-to-consumer channels, owned media, personalization, data use, product roadmap, or CRM/customer relationship management.
6. Include AI/source transparency. Disclose any AI tools used, how they informed the work, and what your team checked, corrected, rejected, or rewrote.

## Evidence Discipline

Students should distinguish:

- Observed: sourced company facts, data points, or assigned case/course facts.
- Inferred: reasonable leadership or operating implications drawn from evidence.
- Recommended / Judged: the team's evaluation of which firm showed stronger alignment between leadership mindset and organizational readiness.

Students should not invent current data. Any public data point must include source and date. AI output is not evidence of current company performance or current company capabilities.

## Strong-Answer Indicators

- Compares leadership interventions rather than summarizing each company separately.
- Applies frameworks to explain the leadership shift.
- Distinguishes mindset from organizational readiness.
- Uses one relevant data point per company with source/date.
- Connects leadership to a digital operating model or touchpoint.
- Makes a clear judgment and defends it.
- Discloses AI/source use transparently.

## Weak-Answer Indicators

- Lists traits without applying them.
- Treats Microsoft and Nike as identical digital transformations.
- Uses vague or uncited performance claims.
- Focuses only on stock price without tying evidence to leadership or readiness.
- Ignores organizational readiness.
- Adds unrelated frameworks.
- Uses AI output as evidence without verification.
