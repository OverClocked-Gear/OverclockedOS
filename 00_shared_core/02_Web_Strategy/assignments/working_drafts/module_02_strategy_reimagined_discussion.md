# Module 2 Discussion: Strategy Reimagined — Adobe Creative Cloud and Kinetic Strategy

## Draft Status

This is an instructor-facing working draft and a light revision of the existing Module 2 discussion. It preserves the Adobe Creative Cloud strategy focus. It is not a Brand Pod and not a new assignment.

## Course Placement

- Course: Web Strategy
- Module: Module 2 — Strategy Re-Imagined
- Audience: undergraduate
- Delivery: asynchronous
- Assessment type: existing team discussion
- Team structure: assigned partner/team of two, per syllabus

## Original Assignment Intent

The original prompt asks students to reassess Adobe's Creative Cloud pivot from perpetual boxed software to a subscription-based digital model. It asks students to apply digital strategy frameworks, connect the analysis to Week 1 leadership concepts, evaluate whether Adobe's strategy was built for adaptability and velocity, and support the analysis with evidence.

## Module Objective Alignment

Module 2 syllabus objectives:

- Analyze how digital transformation has disrupted traditional strategic assumptions.
- Adapt strategic frameworks (SWOT, Porter's Five Forces, Business Model Canvas) to digital contexts.

The revised prompt supports these objectives by asking students to explain why static strategy models are insufficient in digital competition. Adobe's Creative Cloud pivot gives students a concrete example of strategy shifting from product-release planning to a more adaptive operating model shaped by subscription logic, continuous updates, recurring customer relationships, usage signals, and business-model evolution.

## Framework Contract

Required framework:

- Kinetic Strategy Framework

Students must use the Kinetic Strategy Framework to evaluate Adobe's Creative Cloud pivot through:

- Velocity: how quickly Adobe could sense, decide, ship, update, and learn.
- Adaptability: how effectively Adobe reconfigured its business model, customer relationship, revenue model, and operating assumptions.

Optional extensions:

- Digital Horizon Planning
- Digital Business Model Canvas
- Reinvention Strategy Maturity Matrix
- Week 1 leadership concepts, if used to explain why Adobe could execute the strategic shift

Do not force:

- Classic SWOT as the primary analysis
- Porter's Five Forces as the primary analysis
- Full infrastructure stack analysis
- Media/content frameworks
- AI/future-state frameworks

Students may briefly reference SWOT, Porter, or the Business Model Canvas only if they explain how the digital version changes the strategic question. The main answer should not be a traditional SWOT.

Students must use the framework to:

- Place Adobe's pivot in relation to velocity and adaptability.
- Make a strategic judgment.
- Connect the framework to specific Adobe actions or outcomes.
- Avoid defining the framework without applying it.

## Role and Touchpoint Cue

Use a light role lens only:

- Digital Strategy / General Manager
- Analytics & Experimentation as a supporting cue

Students should think like leaders evaluating whether Adobe's strategy created a more adaptive and measurable operating model.

Students should identify one way Adobe's strategic shift likely became visible in a customer or operating experience, such as:

- subscription/pricing model
- Creative Cloud plan or product bundle
- cloud storage or collaboration
- cross-device access
- continuous feature updates
- customer account relationship
- recurring revenue and usage signals
- product adoption or retention measurement

Do not require live website/app inspection.

## Student-Facing Prompt Draft

Adobe's 2013 shift from perpetual boxed software to Creative Cloud is often framed as a major digital strategy pivot. For this discussion, your team will evaluate that pivot using the Kinetic Strategy Framework.

With your assigned partner/team, write a concise response that addresses the following:

1. Briefly explain Adobe's strategic problem. What made the boxed software model less useful in a digital environment?
2. Apply the Kinetic Strategy Framework. Use velocity and adaptability to evaluate Adobe's Creative Cloud pivot.
3. Explain how Adobe's move changed velocity and adaptability. Consider learning cycles, updates, customer relationships, revenue model, or operating assumptions.
4. Compare Adobe's strategy to one traditional strategic assumption that became less useful in a digital context, such as stable industry boundaries, long product cycles, durable barriers to entry, or static competitive positioning.
5. Identify one customer-facing or operating touchpoint where the strategy likely showed up, such as subscription pricing, Creative Cloud plans, cloud storage, cross-device access, customer accounts, continuous updates, usage signals, product adoption, or retention measurement.
6. Use at least one cited source or assigned reading example. If using current public data, include source and date.
7. Include AI/source transparency. Disclose any AI tools used, how they informed the work, and what your team checked, corrected, rejected, or rewrote.

## Evidence Discipline

Students should distinguish:

- Observed: sourced company facts, assigned case/course facts, or public data.
- Inferred: reasonable strategic or operating implications drawn from evidence.
- Judged / Recommended: the team's evaluation of Adobe's strategic position.

Students should not invent current data. Any public data point must include source and date.

The original assignment included an image requirement. Recommend removing it unless the image directly supports the analysis. If retained, the image must be treated as evidence and briefly explained.

## Suggested Response Structure

1. Adobe's strategic problem
2. Kinetic Strategy application
3. Velocity and adaptability analysis
4. Traditional strategy assumption challenged
5. Digital touchpoint or operating implication
6. Evidence/source
7. AI/source disclosure

## Strong-Answer Indicators

- Identifies Adobe's pre-Creative Cloud strategic problem.
- Applies Kinetic Strategy rather than merely naming it.
- Clearly distinguishes velocity from adaptability.
- Explains how subscription/live-service logic changed Adobe's customer relationship.
- Connects strategy to a customer or operating touchpoint.
- Uses evidence from assigned materials or cited public sources.
- Explains why traditional static strategy tools are insufficient.
- Makes a clear strategic judgment.
- Discloses AI/source use transparently.

## Weak-Answer Indicators

- Summarizes Adobe without strategic diagnosis.
- Defines Kinetic Strategy but does not apply it.
- Treats velocity as simply "moving fast."
- Treats adaptability as simply "being flexible."
- Uses classic SWOT or Porter as the main analysis without digital adaptation.
- Makes uncited claims about Adobe performance.
- Includes an image without explaining its analytical relevance.
- Uses AI output as evidence without verification.
