## Module Objective Alignment

Module 10 syllabus objectives:

- Understand the relationship between owned media, content strategy, and customer empowerment.
- Create content strategies specifically designed to earn amplification.
- Develop an integrated paid media strategy that aligns with business objectives and connects effectively with content and earned media.

This activity supports the objectives by asking students to inspect how one public Domino’s digital surface connects media attention to an owned customer experience. Team A examines the paid/earned attention and measurement side: likely traffic intent, conversion path, landing experience, and performance logic. Team B examines the owned experience side: content utility, product or offer clarity, merchandising, lifecycle expectations, loyalty, and relationship continuity. The paired response requires students to connect owned media, content strategy, earned amplification, paid media alignment, customer empowerment, media-to-owned integration, and measurement of digital outcomes.

## Overclocked Framework Connections

Use only the frameworks needed for this activity:

- Owned, earned, and paid media ecosystem: students examine how paid or earned attention should connect to an owned surface the brand controls.
- Content strategy: students evaluate whether the surface helps customers understand value, compare options, and take a useful next action.
- Customer journey / owned experience connection: students connect attention, evaluation, action, and relationship continuity.
- Measurement and experimentation: students propose one metric that fits the observed friction, opportunity, or recommendation.
- Adaptive GTM or media orchestration, where appropriate: students consider how media, content, CRM, and measurement must coordinate around one customer outcome.

Do not overload the assignment with unrelated frameworks or turn it into a full case solution.

## 360° Operating-Role Design

### Team A

Primary lenses:

- Performance Marketing
- Analytics & Experimentation

Performance Marketing owns campaign objective fit, audience and traffic intent, channel-to-surface alignment, paid/earned-to-owned handoff, conversion path quality, and acquisition efficiency. Analytics & Experimentation owns measurement logic, KPI definition, testable hypotheses, instrumentation needs, evidence discipline, and interpretation limits.

Team A must decide which media-to-action issue matters most on the assigned surface and what improvement would better convert attention into measurable owned action. These roles depend on Digital Merchandising & Content for message and offer clarity, CRM/Lifecycle for relationship handoff, Product for flow quality, and Platform/Technology for tracking and page reliability. Success would be measured through a matched metric such as click-to-action, landing-page conversion, offer selection, campaign-to-owned conversion, test lift, ordering-entry progression before login/payment, or another proposed metric tied to the issue.

### Team B

Primary lenses:

- Digital Merchandising & Content
- CRM, Lifecycle & Loyalty

Digital Merchandising & Content owns product or offer presentation, content hierarchy, decision support, trust cues, and content-to-conversion alignment. CRM, Lifecycle & Loyalty owns relationship continuity, customer-stage expectations, opt-in or loyalty handoff, retention logic, preference/consent implications, and ongoing owned relationship value.

Team B must decide what the customer needs to understand, compare, trust, or act on, and what owned relationship improvement should be prioritized. These roles depend on Performance Marketing for traffic intent, Analytics & Experimentation for measurement, Product for experience feasibility, and Platform/Technology for execution dependencies. Success would be measured through a matched metric such as offer comprehension, product/menu engagement, content-assisted conversion, opt-in or loyalty-start progression where publicly appropriate, repeat-visit intent, or another proposed metric tied to the recommendation.

### Paired-Team Synthesis

The paired reply uses Digital Strategy / General Manager as the integration lens. Students must reconcile media performance and owned customer experience into one combined priority.

These role pairings are complementary and appropriate for Module 10 because the module sits at the intersection of paid, earned, owned, content, CRM, and measurement. Team A asks whether attention becomes measurable action. Team B asks whether the owned surface earns continued relationship value. Product and Platform/Technology are not primary lenses for this pilot unless they are needed as dependencies.

## Evidence Discipline

Students must label key claims as:

- Observed: directly visible on the assigned public surface or documented in assigned course materials
- Inferred: a reasonable media, content, CRM, measurement, role, process, or operating dependency
- Recommended: the team’s proposed action

Students must not claim to know Domino’s paid media targeting, internal analytics, campaign performance, CRM rules, personalization logic, customer data, loyalty economics, vendor systems, or internal systems unless provided in assigned course material. App-store descriptions may be used only as supporting evidence, not as direct observation of the in-app experience.

## Team A Assignment

Team A uses Performance Marketing plus Analytics & Experimentation.

The team should:

1. Identify the likely traffic or attention source the surface is designed to receive.
2. Identify one observed conversion, offer, clarity, friction, or measurement issue.
3. Explain what Performance Marketing owns.
4. Explain what Analytics/Experimentation owns.
5. Identify one dependency between media, landing experience, tracking, testing, or measurement.
6. Recommend one specific improvement to better connect attention to action.
7. Propose one matched metric and explain why it fits.
8. Apply the relevant media ecosystem and measurement concepts.

Keep the analysis grounded in public evidence and reasonable inference.

## Team B Assignment

Team B uses Digital Merchandising & Content plus CRM, Lifecycle & Loyalty.

The team should:

1. Use the same assigned public surface.
2. Identify what the customer needs to understand, compare, trust, or act on.
3. Explain what Merchandising/Content owns.
4. Explain what CRM/Lifecycle owns.
5. Identify one dependency between content, offer clarity, product presentation, email/SMS/push/lifecycle expectations, loyalty, or owned relationship development.
6. Recommend one specific improvement to strengthen the owned customer relationship.
7. Propose one matched metric and explain why it fits.
8. Apply the relevant content, owned media, and lifecycle concepts.

Keep the analysis focused on content utility, product/offer presentation, relationship continuity, and owned media, not visual preference alone.

## Strong-Answer Indicators

- Identifies a specific media-to-owned relationship problem or opportunity.
- Connects traffic intent to landing/owned surface quality.
- Uses media ecosystem, content, owned media, lifecycle, and measurement concepts accurately.
- Explains what each assigned role owns.
- Identifies a meaningful dependency between media, content, CRM, and analytics.
- Distinguishes direct observation from reasonable inference.
- Avoids unsupported claims about paid targeting, CRM logic, or internal analytics.
- Recommends one feasible improvement.
- Selects a metric that directly matches the recommendation.
- Synthesizes media performance and owned customer relationship priorities.

## Weak-Answer Indicators

- Focuses only on visual critique.
- Makes unsupported claims about paid media or CRM targeting.
- Uses vague metrics such as “engagement” without defining the action or outcome.
- Fails to connect media attention to owned experience.
- Proposes a generic recommendation.
- Names frameworks without applying them.
- Treats loyalty only as discounts.
- Treats app-store descriptions as direct evidence of in-app experience.
- Treats paid media as ending at the click.
- Treats the paired reply as agreement rather than synthesis.

## Common Student Misconceptions

- Paid media ends at the click.
- Content is just copy/design.
- Merchandising only applies to retail products.
- CRM is only email.
- Analytics is only reporting.
- Loyalty is only discounts.
- A metric must already be public.
- Engagement is always the best metric.
